# Game Structure: Two Acts

> The game is two acts separated by the nunnery transition.
> Which prince sits the throne determines everything about Act 2.

---

## Overview

```
ACT 1: Taizong's Court
  You are 19, a Cairen (Rank 9), one of dozens.
  Emperor Taizong is a legendary warrior-emperor in declining health.
  The real game: the three princes and the succession.
  
  Win condition: Have a living son OR have a prince who will rescue you.
  Fail condition: Taizong dies and you have neither → nunnery → game over.

         ┌─────────── Taizong dies ───────────┐
         │                                     │
    Has a son?  ──── yes ──→  Dowager path    │
         │                    (powerful start   │
         no                    to Act 2)        │
         │                                     │
    Prince favor? ── yes ──→  Nunnery (brief) ─→ Recalled by prince
         │                                     
         no                                    
         │                                     
      GAME OVER                               
      (sent to nunnery, forgotten)             

ACT 2: The New Emperor's Court
  The prince who took the throne determines:
    - The political landscape
    - Which factions have power
    - What kind of ruler you're dealing with
    - Your starting position and relationships
    - Available endings
```

---

## Act 1: Taizong's Court

### The Emperor

**Emperor Taizong** (太宗 李世民) — Jade tier
- Age: Late 40s, declining
- Stats: Beauty 2, Cunning 5, Eloquence 4, Discretion 3, Resolve 5, Vitality 3→declining, Resourcefulness 4, Scholarship 4, Spiritual Arts 2
- **Personality:** The greatest emperor in Chinese history and he knows it. Brilliant general, shrewd politician, patron of arts. He founded the dynasty through a palace coup (killing his own brothers). He's surrounded by the best minds of the age — he doesn't suffer fools. He barely notices low-ranking concubines. Getting his attention is genuinely hard.
- **Romance path:** It IS possible to become Taizong's favorite, but it requires exceptional stats and specific event chains. He respects intelligence and boldness over beauty alone (the Lion Stallion anecdote). If you succeed, Act 2 is radically different — you're a powerful dowager, not a discarded nobody.
- **Health:** Taizong historically became obsessed with Taoist longevity elixirs (which likely poisoned him). His health declines over the course of Act 1. The player can observe this, try to help (physician events), or exploit it. The exact day of death is somewhat random — the player knows he's declining but not when the end comes.

### The Three Princes

All three are at court during Act 1. They attend events, have their own storylines, and compete for succession. The player can cultivate relationships with one or more of them.

---

#### Prince Chengqian (李承乾) — "The Crown Prince"

**Title:** Crown Prince (太子), heir apparent
**Tier:** Gold
**Age:** Mid 20s
**Tags:** male, imperial

**Stats:** Beauty 3, Cunning 3, Eloquence 3, Discretion 1, Resolve 2, Vitality 2, Resourcefulness 2, Scholarship 3, Spiritual Arts 1

**Personality:** Tormented. He's the designated heir and he hates it. He's expected to be a copy of his legendary father and he knows he'll never measure up. He's developed a rebellious streak — Turkic culture, hunting, reckless behavior. He has a male lover, a musician named Cheng Xinnu (称心), whom he keeps semi-secretly. The court whispers. His father is in denial.

He's not stupid — he's wounded. If someone shows him genuine kindness without agenda, he opens up. But he's also capable of reckless, self-destructive decisions (historically, he plotted a coup out of desperation).

**Relationship path:**
- **Friendship/Confidant:** He doesn't want a lover from the harem — he wants someone who understands. A genuine friendship with him is possible and valuable. He confides his fears, his love for Xinnu, his hatred of court life.
- **Political ally:** Support his position as heir. Help him manage his image. Cover for his relationship. In return, if he becomes emperor, you're his most trusted advisor.
- **The risk:** He may self-destruct regardless. Supporting him means possibly going down with him.

**If he becomes Emperor:**
- An unconventional court. He doesn't care about the harem system. You're an advisor, not a consort. He gives you real political power but zero romantic attention.
- The Confucian establishment is horrified. Constant political crises.
- His lover Xinnu is at court openly. Scandal is perpetual.
- Unique endings available: progressive reform, cultural revolution, or spectacular collapse.

**Historical basis:** Li Chengqian was deposed in 643 for plotting rebellion after his father increasingly favored Li Tai. His male lover Cheng Xinnu was discovered and executed by Taizong, which broke Chengqian. In this game, the player can potentially prevent this chain of events.

---

#### Prince Tai (李泰) — "The Scholar Prince"

**Title:** Prince of Wei (魏王)
**Tier:** Gold
**Age:** Early 20s
**Tags:** male, imperial

**Stats:** Beauty 2, Cunning 5, Eloquence 5, Discretion 4, Resolve 3, Vitality 2, Resourcefulness 3, Scholarship 5, Spiritual Arts 2

**Personality:** Brilliant and dangerous. He compiled a massive geographic encyclopedia (the Kuodi Zhi) with a team of scholars — a genuine intellectual achievement. He's his father's favorite in terms of talent. But beneath the scholarly surface is a cold, calculating ambition. He wants the throne and he's willing to manipulate anyone to get it.

He's charming when he wants to be. He flatters, he gifts, he impresses. He's also willing to fabricate evidence against his brother, lie to his father's face, and discard allies once they're used up. He's the most dangerous person at court who isn't the Emperor.

**Relationship path:**
- **Romance:** He's attracted to intelligent women. He'll pursue you if your Scholarship and Cunning are high enough. But he sees you as a tool — a beautiful, clever tool. The romance is passionate but you'll always wonder if it's real.
- **Alliance:** Work together to undermine Chengqian and position Tai as heir. You do the inner-court work, he does the outer-court work. A genuine partnership of schemers.
- **The risk:** He promised his father he'd kill his own sons to secure the succession for Chengqian's line. He lies. He'll promise you anything.

**If he becomes Emperor:**
- A meritocratic, intellectual court — but also paranoid. He trusts no one fully.
- He respects you as a political partner. The harem is a chess board.
- He's actually competent at governing. The challenge is surviving his suspicion.
- Unique endings: enlightened despotism, betrayal from within, knowledge empire.

**Historical basis:** Li Tai was passed over for the throne precisely because Taizong realized he was too scheming. Taizong chose Li Zhi specifically because "Zhi is weak — he will not harm his brothers." In this game, the player can change that calculus.

---

#### Prince Zhi (李治) — "The Gentle Prince"

**Title:** Prince of Jin (晋王), ninth son
**Tier:** Silver → Gold
**Age:** Late teens / early 20s
**Tags:** male, imperial

**Stats:** Beauty 3, Cunning 2, Eloquence 4, Discretion 2, Resolve 1, Vitality 2, Resourcefulness 2, Scholarship 4, Spiritual Arts 3

**Personality:** Gentle, romantic, genuinely kind. He loves poetry, Buddhist philosophy, and quiet conversation. He is not built for power — and everyone knows it, including him. He's the youngest serious contender and he never expected to be emperor.

He falls in love easily and deeply. He's drawn to strong women because he knows he's weak. He's the one who will visit you in the nunnery. He's the one who will cry when his father dies. He's the one who will give you real power because he genuinely cannot handle it alone.

He is also, quietly, capable of stubbornness when something matters to him. He defied the entire court to make Wu empress — not because he was manipulated, but because he loved her and chose her over everything else. Whether that's strength or weakness depends on your perspective.

**Relationship path:**
- **Romance:** The most emotionally rewarding path — but also the hardest to initiate. He's a ninth son. He doesn't attend the same events as you. He's in the Imperial Library studying Buddhist texts while you're fighting for scraps in the inner court. Creating opportunities to even MEET him requires deliberate effort — Scholarship events, temple visits, poetry circles where a junior prince might appear. Then: cultivate a genuine connection through shared interests (poetry, philosophy, kindness). He falls slowly but completely.
- **Engineering his succession:** Even if Zhi loves you, he won't become emperor unless both brothers are out. You need to either: let Chengqian self-destruct + expose Tai's schemes, or actively sabotage both. This is a multi-front campaign while being a Rank 9 nobody.
- **Manipulation vs. love:** You can use his feelings cynically. He's trusting. Dilemma: do you love him back, or are you using the one person at court who's genuinely good?
- **The risk:** He's weak. His health will decline. You'll eventually have to rule for him — or through him. And the court will never fully accept it. You chose the hardest path because it leads to the richest story.

**If he becomes Emperor (= the historical path):**
- You have maximum romantic influence but minimum institutional power (at first).
- The old guard (Zhangsun Wuji) tries to control him. You must outmaneuver them.
- His declining health is Act 2's ticking clock.
- This is the path with the most storyline content (it's the historical path).
- Unique endings: Empress, Curtain Governance, Zhou Dynasty, The Wordless Stele.

**Historical basis:** Li Zhi was chosen as heir specifically because he was seen as harmless. He and Wu likely began their affair while Taizong was still alive and bedridden. He became Emperor Gaozong and Wu dominated his reign.

### Act 1 Timeline

| Phase | Days | What's happening |
|---|---|---|
| **Arrival** | 1–8 | Enter the palace. Learn hierarchy. Meet the princes at a distance. First edict from Taizong. |
| **Establishment** | 9–18 | Build position among consorts. Choose which prince(s) to cultivate. First signs of Taizong's declining health. |
| **The Succession Crisis** | 19–28 | Princes maneuvering openly. Chengqian's relationship exposed? Tai's schemes revealed? Zhi quietly rising? |
| **The Decline** | 28–?? | Taizong visibly ill. Elixir obsession. The court holds its breath. Death is coming but when? |
| **The Death** | Random (30–40) | Taizong dies. Succession decided by accumulated events. Nunnery or not. |

### Act 1 Key Mechanics

**Prince Favor** — Three separate tracks (like reputation but for each prince):
- Build through events, gifts, conversations, political support
- Each prince values different things (Chengqian: loyalty/acceptance, Tai: intelligence/usefulness, Zhi: beauty/emotional connection)
- You can cultivate multiple princes but spreading thin is risky

**Difficulty by prince path:**

| Path | Difficulty | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Chengqian** | Easier entry | He's Crown Prince — backing the heir is natural. The challenge is keeping him from self-destructing. His relationship with Xinnu is a time bomb. You're managing damage, not building from scratch. |
| **Tai** | Easier entry | He actively recruits useful people — he'll approach YOU if your stats are good. The challenge is Act 2: surviving his paranoia. Easy to get in, hard to stay alive. |
| **Zhi** | Hard | Ninth son, no claim, no ambition, overlooked by everyone. You have to: (1) build a relationship with a prince nobody is watching, (2) engineer the downfall or disqualification of BOTH elder brothers, (3) convince Taizong that the gentle son is the safe choice, (4) do all this as a Rank 9 Cairen with no power. This is the historical path, the canon run, and the path with the richest Act 2 content. Earning it should feel like an achievement. |
| **Taizong romance** | Hardest | Taizong is a legend. You're one of dozens of concubines. Getting his sustained attention requires exceptional stats and bold moves. But if you succeed: you skip the nunnery entirely and enter Act 2 as a dowager with a son. The ultimate shortcut — that's almost impossible to pull off. |

**Taizong Romance** — A separate, harder path:
- Taizong values boldness, intelligence, and spirit (not just beauty)
- Requires high Cunning + Eloquence + Resolve
- If successful: you bear Taizong's child → you're a dowager empress in Act 2 regardless of which prince takes over
- But: romancing Taizong alienates the princes (you're their father's woman)

**The Heir Question** — Player actions influence who becomes emperor:
- Support Chengqian → he holds the throne (if you help him avoid self-destruction)
- Support Tai → he outmaneuvers Chengqian (if you help him build the coalition)
- Support Zhi → the "safe choice" that Taizong makes when neither elder son is viable
- Do nothing → Taizong decides based on circumstances (semi-random based on game state)
- Actively sabotage a prince → remove him from contention

**Pregnancy** — The ultimate insurance:
- A living son by Taizong = you can never be fully discarded
- Pregnancy events: Vitality checks, protection from rivals who might cause a "miscarriage"
- Having a son by a prince (secretly) is also possible but incredibly risky

## The Nunnery (Transition)

If you don't have a living son by Taizong, you're sent to Ganye Temple with the other childless concubines. Brief gameplay:

- 3–5 events. Buddhist study, temple life, depression or resilience.
- A prince with sufficient favor sends a messenger.
- You're recalled to court.
- If no prince has enough favor: **game over.** The nunnery is permanent.
- The nunnery is NOT a full act. It's a transition — filler with emotional weight.

## Act 2: The New Emperor's Court

Act 2 plays differently depending on who sits the throne.

### If Chengqian is Emperor

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Your role** | Political advisor, not romantic partner. He values your counsel. |
| **Court atmosphere** | Chaotic, unconventional. He challenges every tradition. |
| **Primary conflict** | Confucian backlash. The establishment wants him gone. |
| **Rivals** | Conservative officials, his brothers plotting from exile. |
| **Unique mechanic** | "Reform vs. Tradition" tension replaces some harem intrigue. |
| **Available endings** | Progressive utopia, palace coup, cultural renaissance, exile. |

### If Tai is Emperor

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Your role** | Partner in power. He respects you but trusts no one. |
| **Court atmosphere** | Meritocratic but paranoid. Purges are frequent. |
| **Primary conflict** | His own paranoia. He sees threats everywhere. |
| **Rivals** | Anyone who gets too powerful — including you. |
| **Unique mechanic** | "Trust" meter with Tai. It erodes naturally. Maintain it or be purged. |
| **Available endings** | Enlightened despotism, mutual destruction, scholarly golden age, betrayal. |

### If Zhi is Emperor (Historical Path)

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Your role** | The power behind the throne. Romantic partner becoming political ruler. |
| **Court atmosphere** | Traditional but with a weak center. Everyone senses the vacuum. |
| **Primary conflict** | The old guard (Zhangsun Wuji) vs. you. Then: your own children vs. you. |
| **Rivals** | Empress Wang, Consort Xiao, then the chancellors, then your own sons. |
| **Unique mechanic** | Zhi's declining health + Curtain Governance progression. |
| **Available endings** | Empress, Zhou Dynasty, The Wordless Stele, Mother of Emperors, all death endings. |
| **Note** | This is the path with the most content — it follows history. |

### If You're Taizong's Dowager

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Your role** | Powerful but constrained. Mother of a prince, not wife of the new emperor. |
| **Court atmosphere** | You have institutional power but no romantic leverage with the new emperor. |
| **Primary conflict** | Using your son's claim to maintain influence. The new emperor may resent you. |
| **Unique mechanic** | "Dowager Influence" — a decaying resource. You start high and must fight to maintain. |
| **Available endings** | Regent, Kingmaker, Honored Retirement, Power Struggle. |

## Act 2 Characters

The Act 1 princes who didn't become emperor are still present:
- **The emperor's brothers** are potential allies, rivals, or victims
- Their fates from Act 1 carry forward (exiled? imprisoned? loyal?)
- Act 2 introduces new characters specific to each emperor's court

The consorts from Act 1 who survived carry their relationships forward.
Chunhua's resentment carries forward.
Your reputation carries forward.
Your doctrine path carries forward.

Everything compounds.
