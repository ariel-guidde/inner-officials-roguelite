## Starting Plotlines

Three narrative threads are active from **Day 1** of every run. They introduce core mechanics through story.

---

### Plotline 1 — The Wu Letter (家书)

*A sealed letter from your father sits on your writing table. He must have sent it before you even boarded the palanquin.*

The letter arrives **Day 3**. Your father writes warmly — and then asks. Your cousin Deng needs a minor post in the palace supply office. A small favor. It shouldn't be complicated.

**Mechanics**: Generates a Bronze-tier Dominion edict on Day 5. Failing it loses 2 Wu Family Standing. Completing it grants 3 Standing + your father sends 2 silver in a follow-up letter.

*This introduces: Dominion edicts, Wu Family Standing, the tension between family obligations and self-preservation.*

---

### Plotline 2 — The Watching Consort (眼神)

*At your first Morning Court, you notice a woman in faded Zhaoyi robes watching you with careful eyes. She does not speak. She does not look away.*

She is **Consort Shen** — formerly beloved, now fading. She was where you are, once. She may be a warning, a mentor, or a trap.

**Day 4**: She sends a gift with no message.
**Day 7**: She makes contact.

Her storyline has three branches determined by your Cunning stat:
- **Cunning 4+**: You read the situation correctly — she is a genuine ally. She becomes a Bronze Scholar agent (Cunning 4, Scholarship 3) with intel on a senior rival.
- **Cunning 2–3**: You can't be sure. A risky Discretion check reveals her intent.
- **Below threshold**: She turns out to be the Empress's plant — a carefully laid test. Responding without caution generates Slander +1.

*This introduces: Court politics, agent recruitment, the risk of trust.*

---

### Plotline 3 — Chunhua's Trouble (春花心事)

*Chunhua is distracted. You notice it the second morning — she keeps glancing toward the east corridor guard post.*

She won't say directly. A Cunning or Eloquence check gets her to admit: a guard has been following her. Not dangerously. He seems interested. She doesn't know how she feels.

The guard is **Private Chen** — Clay-tier Guard, Resolve 2, Vitality 2, **Martial 1**. Harmless for now.

Your response seeds Chunhua's early loyalty state:
- Encourage her carefully → Resentment −0, Chen becomes a potential Clay recruit later
- Forbid contact → Resentment +1, Chen becomes resentful toward you (potential problem)
- Ignore it entirely → Resentment +1 (she notices you didn't care), situation develops on its own

*This introduces: The Resentment mechanic and its low-stakes early form. Also: that Clay-tier guards are real people with uses and feelings.*

## Background Plotlines

One additional plotline per background, active from Day 1:

---

### Scholar's Daughter — "The Forbidden Text"

*Before you left home, your father took your hands and said: the east wing of the Imperial Library has a sealed section. Do not read what is locked there. He would not say why.*

**Day 6**: The east wing is temporarily unsupervised. The lock is old.

- **Open it**: Gain +2 Scholarship permanently, +2 Heavenly Sight, begin a slow-burn supernatural plotline. Your father's next letter is troubled.
- **Walk away**: Gain +1 Virtue. Your father sends a brief letter of relief. The text remains a mystery that may resurface later.

---

### General's Daughter — "The Father's Regiment"

*Sergeant Luo tells you quietly, on Day 2: a guard in your wing served under your father's banner. His name is Liu Tao. He was discharged dishonorably — but he claims he was framed.*

A Cunning + Discretion investigation event (2 days):
- **Innocent**: He becomes a loyal Bronze Guard (Resolve 3, Vitality 3, Martial 3). He is grateful in a way that doesn't ask for anything.
- **Guilty**: He is a plant by a rival. Discovering this yields 2 Intelligence Scrolls and Shadow Reach +1. He is quietly removed.

---

### Merchant's Daughter — "The Debt"

*Cousin Bao pulls you aside Day 2. The family's supply arrangement with a palace provisioner has gone sour. Someone on the inside is undercutting their prices. If it continues, the family loses their capital foothold.*

A Dominion sub-edict (Cunning + Shadow Reach, 3 days): identify and neutralize the competing supplier.

- **Success**: Passive income from Trade Quarter (2 silver per 7 days) begins.
- **Failure**: Cousin Bao returns home to manage the crisis personally. You lose him as an agent.

---

### Beauty of the Province — "The Poem"

*On Day 1, a page brings a folded poem. Unsigned. Extraordinarily good. It describes your eyes as you entered the Morning Court — written by someone watching very carefully.*

Discretion + Cunning determines what you discover:
- **High roll**: The writer is the Emperor himself, testing if you're interesting. Day 3 Morning Court: he looks at you differently. Favour +2.
- **Medium roll**: A senior scholar who wants a patron. Recruitable as Bronze Scholar (Eloquence 3, Scholarship 4) if you respond warmly.
- **Low roll**: A rival consort testing whether you'll respond inappropriately to unknown admirers. Replying carelessly: Slander +1.

---

### Temple Raised — "The Omen"

*Your prayer beads feel warm the first evening. At meditation, you see clearly: a red door somewhere in the palace you haven't found yet. Behind it — running water and a woman weeping.*

Over 5 days, clues surface during Spiritual Arts checks. The red door is in the Eunuch Quarter.

- **Follow the visions**: Rescue a concubine being held illegally. Virtue +3. She becomes a Silver-tier Monk agent (Spiritual Arts 5, Scholarship 3) — the strongest spiritual agent in the game.
- **Ignore the visions**: Heavenly Sight stagnates. The woman's fate remains unknown. The beads never feel warm again.
