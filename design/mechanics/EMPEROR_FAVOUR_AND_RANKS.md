# Emperor's Favour & Concubine Ranks

These two mechanics are the central axis of the game. Everything — events, politics, scandals, resources — feeds into or is shaped by your position in the Emperor's personal regard and your official standing in the harem hierarchy.

---

## Imperial Favour (圣眷)

Imperial Favour is a **numerical resource (0–50)** representing the Emperor's personal regard for you. It is **not** the same as your official rank — a low-ranking concubine can hold the Emperor's deep affection while a senior consort is politically powerful but personally ignored. Both matter. Neither replaces the other.

### Favour Bands

| Band | Range | State | Mechanical Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Forgotten** (皇失忆) | 0–5 | Not on the Emperor's radar | No bedchamber summons; slander sticks without check |
| **Noticed** (初引目) | 6–10 | Known by name | Occasional event invitations; 1 Slander can be dispelled per court attendance |
| **Favored** (承恩) | 11–20 | Invited for visits | Regular bedchamber summons possible; rank advancement unlocked; slander requires 2 Slander to trigger Rebuke instead of 3 when you have allies |
| **Beloved** (得宠) | 21–30 | Consistent attention | Rivals begin open scheming; can make minor requests of the Emperor; Empress begins passive interference |
| **Cherished** (深得圣心) | 31–40 | Palace whispers your name | Strong protection from accusations; Emperor publicly shows preference; Empress treats you as a threat |
| **Paramount** (独宠) | 41–50 | Unprecedented | Empress moves against you directly; rivals form coalitions; can request senior appointments for Wu allies; Jade-tier crisis events may trigger |

### Gaining Favour

| Action | Favour Gained |
|---|---|
| Successful bedchamber visit | +3 |
| Winning a court performance (poetry, music, dance) | +2 |
| Giving a well-chosen gift to the Emperor | +1 to +3 (gift quality-based) |
| Producing a male imperial heir | +8 |
| Fulfilling a Favour-type Edict successfully | +4 |
| Being publicly recommended by the Empress Dowager | +3 |
| Winning a beauty contest against rival consorts | +2 |
| Receiving a Virtue title (public ceremony) | +2 |

### Losing Favour

| Event | Favour Lost |
|---|---|
| Imperial Rebuke (3 Slander triggered) | −5 |
| Failed or boring bedchamber visit | −1 |
| Public disgrace or humiliation event | −3 |
| Being implicated in a Witchcraft Accusation scandal | −4 |
| Favour decay (no Emperor interaction for 14+ days) | −1 per 7 days |
| A rival consort produces a son while you have not | −2 |
| Being demoted in rank | −3 |

### Favour as Protection

Favour acts as a shield against slander and accusations:
- **Favored (11+)**: The Emperor gives you the benefit of the doubt. Dispel 1 Slander automatically per court appearance.
- **Beloved (21+)**: Accused of minor scandals? The Emperor dismisses them without requiring your defense.
- **Cherished (31+)**: Only Gold-tier agents can slander you effectively. Lower-rank consorts' whispers are brushed aside.
- **Paramount (41+)**: The Empress must work through back channels; direct accusation is politically costly for her.

## Bedchamber Visits (侍寝)

The Emperor's bedchamber is only entered **by Imperial Summons** — you cannot simply appear. Each visit is a major event with lasting consequences.

### Getting Summoned

The Emperor selects from eligible consorts based on:
- Your current Favour band (must be **Favored or higher** for regular summons)
- Your **Beauty** stat (directly increases selection probability)
- Recent **court performances** (impressive events raise your prominence in the selection pool)
- **Rivalry interference**: the Empress or other senior consorts can block your summons (Dominion/Cunning-based actions)

### The Visit — Three Checks

When summoned, a sequential event triggers:

| Check | Stats Used | Success | Failure |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Presentation** | Beauty + Vitality | Favour +1, proceed to next check | −1 Favour, event ends early |
| **Conversation** | Eloquence + Scholarship | Favour +1, proceed | Visit ends — pleasant but forgettable |
| **Intimacy** | Beauty + Vitality | Favour +3 total, pregnancy roll triggered | Favour +1 only |

**Optional support**: Assign Chunhua to preparation duty (Beauty +2 on the first check, but she must be free that day).

**Critical Success** (all three checks pass with surplus dice): Favour +5 total, +1 permanent Beauty from the Emperor's attention.

### Pregnancy

After a successful intimacy check, roll for pregnancy:
- Base chance: **20%** (modified by Vitality and active blessings/items)
- Pregnancy confirmed: the **Pregnancy Arc** begins — a multi-week protective event chain
- Announcement triggers immediate rival hostility. Other consorts will attempt to end the pregnancy (poison, curse, engineered accidents).
- A male heir grants Favour +8 and dramatically shifts the political landscape.
- A female heir grants Favour +2 and moderate political benefit.

## Concubine Ranks (宫廷品阶)

Your rank is your **official standing** in the palace hierarchy. It determines daily resources, location access, political weight, and which rivals bother to target you.

### The 9 Ranks

| Rank | Title | Servants Allocated | Daily Silver | Key Access Unlocked |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 9 | **才人 Cairen** — Talented Lady | 1 (Chunhua only) | 1 | Shared quarters; Morning Court; Imperial Library |
| 8 | **美人 Meiren** — Beauty | 2 | 2 | Own small chamber; 1 personal maid assigned |
| 7 | **婕妤 Jieyu** — Handsome Fairness | 3 | 3 | Imperial Gardens access |
| 6 | **昭仪 Zhaoyi** — Lady of Bright Deportment | 4 | 4 | Own palace wing; full Estate Management |
| 5 | **充媛 Chongyuan** — Lady of Full Grace | 6 | 5 | Can sponsor Wu family court appointments |
| 4 | **贵嫔 Guipin** — Noble Consort | 8 | 7 | Inner council voice; can petition Emperor directly |
| 3 | **淑妃 Shufei** — Virtuous Consort | 10 | 10 | One of the Four Consorts; formal political power |
| 2 | **贵妃 Guifei** — Imperial Consort | 14 | 14 | Commands junior consorts; Empress must negotiate with you |
| 1 | **皇后 Huanghou** — Empress | Unlimited | N/A | Rules the inner palace — Standard Win condition |

### Rank Advancement

To advance one rank, you need **both conditions met simultaneously**:

1. **Favour Threshold** — your current Imperial Favour must be at or above the required band
2. **Rank Edict** — a specific edict must be completed demonstrating your worthiness

| Advancing To | Favour Required | Rank Edict Required |
|---|---|---|
| Rank 8 (Meiren) | Noticed (6+) | Any Clay-tier Ceremony or Favour edict |
| Rank 7 (Jieyu) | Favored (11+) | Bronze-tier Ceremony edict |
| Rank 6 (Zhaoyi) | Favored (15+) | Bronze-tier Dominion edict |
| Rank 5 (Chongyuan) | Beloved (21+) | Silver-tier Favour edict |
| Rank 4 (Guipin) | Beloved (25+) | Silver-tier Dominion edict |
| Rank 3 (Shufei) | Cherished (31+) | Gold-tier edict (any type) |
| Rank 2 (Guifei) | Cherished (38+) | Gold-tier Dominion edict + a rival consort must be disgraced/removed |
| Rank 1 (Empress) | Paramount (45+) | Jade-tier "Ascension" edict chain (end-game only) |

Rank ceremonies are **public events**. Every advancement above rank 6 triggers at least one rival consort to escalate hostilities. The ceremony itself is a multi-check event (Eloquence + Beauty) — a failure doesn't cancel the advancement but generates 1 Slander.

### Demotion

You can lose rank through:
- **Imperial Rebuke** (severe outcome): drop 1 rank
- **Losing a Gold-tier political battle**: drop 1 rank
- **Empress's Formal Petition**: she can petition the Emperor to demote consorts below rank 5; requires she has 3+ court supporters
- **Unresolved Gold-tier Scandal** (3+ days overdue): demotion risk becomes an active event

Demotion costs −3 Imperial Favour on top of the rank loss.

## The Empress as Counter-Force

The Empress (皇后) is a Gold-tier antagonist with her own agents, resources, and agenda. She is not simply a rival consort — she is the ruler of the inner palace, and she has been doing this longer than you.

### Escalation Stages

| Your Favour Band | Empress's Response |
|---|---|
| Forgotten (0–5) | Ignores you entirely |
| Noticed (6–10) | Observes you; begins gathering information |
| Favored (11–20) | Subtle interference — a summons blocked, a servant reassigned |
| Beloved (21–30) | Passive agent deployment — Slander generated, your allies approached |
| Cherished (31–40) | Active campaign — staged accidents, formal accusations, restricting your wing's resources |
| Paramount (41–50) | Direct assault — Jade-tier crisis events; she moves to have you exiled or executed |

### Managing the Empress

- **Deference**: Perform public acts of submission. Slows her hostility by 1 stage but costs Favour −1. Sustainable short-term.
- **Alliance through Common Enemy**: Find a rival she also wants removed. Redirect her aggression temporarily.
- **Undermining her support base**: Long game — expose her allies, reduce her court support count, eventually initiate the "Topple the Empress" Jade edict.
- **Empress Dowager's Protection**: If your Virtue reaches 10+, the Dowager can formally restrain the Empress from taking direct action against you.

## Harem Politics Matrix

As you rise, the landscape of who threatens you and whom you can leverage shifts:

| Your Rank | Who Targets You | Your Leverage Over |
|---|---|---|
| 9–8 | Ignored by anyone above rank 7 | No one yet |
| 7–6 | Rivals at rank 7–8 testing you | Rank 9 consorts defer to you |
| 5–4 | Silver-tier faction leaders; Empress begins watching | You can suppress ranks 7–9 |
| 3–2 | The Empress engages directly | You command ranks 5–9 as a faction |
| 1 | Empress Dowager may object; external pressures | You rule the inner palace |
