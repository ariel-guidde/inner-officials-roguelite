# Events & Scandals

> Sultan's Game equivalent: [Event System](../sultans-game-reference/COMPLETE_MECHANICS.md#event-system) and [Sultan Card System](../sultans-game-reference/COMPLETE_MECHANICS.md#sultan-card-system)

## The Edict Deck — What Drives Each Run

In Sultan's Game, you draw from a 28-card deck of "Sultan Cards" that must be broken. In Inner Officials, the equivalent is the **Imperial Edict Deck** (圣旨牌堆) — but the fiction is broader than just edicts from the Emperor.

These are **crises, demands, and obligations** that the concubine must resolve. They come from multiple sources:

| Source | Examples | Feel |
|---|---|---|
| **Imperial Edicts** (圣旨) | The Emperor demands a poetry performance, requests a banquet, orders an investigation | Top-down pressure |
| **Palace Scandals** (宫闱丑闻) | A consort is caught with a guard, poisoning rumors, a eunuch theft ring exposed | Crises you must navigate |
| **Family Obligations** (家族之责) | Wu clan needs a favor, a cousin seeks appointment, family honor is challenged | Pulled from outside |
| **Rival Schemes** (宿敌阴谋) | A consort frames you, assassination attempt, your agent is compromised | Defensive survival |
| **Seasonal Demands** (时令要务) | Festival preparation, harvest ceremonies, the Emperor's birthday | Calendar-driven |

### Edict Tiers (same 5 tiers as agents)

| Tier | Difficulty | Timer | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Clay** (陶) | Easy | 7 days | Arrange flowers for a ceremony, deliver a message, attend a minor gathering |
| **Bronze** (铜) | Moderate | 7 days | Compose a poem for the Emperor, cure a consort's headache, mediate a servant dispute |
| **Silver** (银) | Hard | 7 days | Host a palace banquet, expose a theft ring, secure an imperial pregnancy, win a poetry contest |
| **Gold** (金) | Very Hard | 7 days | Topple a rival consort's faction, survive a purge, birth and protect an imperial heir |
| **Jade** (玉) | Extreme | 7 days | Dethrone the Empress, prevent a palace coup, secure Wu family's place in history |

Resolving an edict requires events of matching or higher tier + agents of matching or higher tier.

### Edict Types (4 categories)

| Type | Chinese | Theme | Events That Resolve It |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Ceremony** (典仪) | Display, culture, spectacle | Host a festival, present a gift, stage a performance, build a garden feature |
| **Favor** (恩宠) | Romance, the Emperor's attention, heirs | Secure a bedchamber visit, win a beauty contest, pregnancy events, romantic spectacles |
| **Dominion** (权域) | Political power, territory, appointments | Gain palace wing control, defeat a faction, secure an appointment for an ally, court victories |
| **Justice** (刑罚) | Investigation, punishment, survival | Expose a traitor, survive an accusation, endure interrogation, investigate poisoning |

## Daily Events

Events appear on the palace map each day. You assign agents to resolve them.

### Event Slot Rules
- **Red slots** (朱槽): Mandatory — all must be filled to begin the event
- **White slots** (白槽): Optional — provide bonus checks, alternate outcomes, or safety nets
- Slots may have **tag requirements**: "Eunuch only", "Noble only", "Female only", "Bronze+ only"
- Agents assigned to multi-day events are **Involved** (锁定) and unavailable

### Recurring Daily Events

| Event | Location | Function | Check |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Morning Court** (朝会) | Inner Court | Political maneuvering, Favor/Slander | Eloquence + Cunning |
| **Estate Management** (管家) | Your Chambers | Silver income | Cunning + Eloquence |
| **Garden Stroll** (游园) | Imperial Gardens | Intelligence gathering, chance encounters | Discretion + Beauty |
| **Library Study** (读书) | Imperial Library | Buy scrolls (stat boosts) | 1 silver, no check |
| **Temple Meditation** (禅修) | Buddhist Temple | Heavenly Sight gain, spiritual events | Spiritual Arts |

### Timed Events (1-3 day deadlines)

These appear with a red countdown and vanish if ignored (sometimes with consequences):
- **Summons**: The Emperor or Empress calls for you (ignoring = Imperial Favor loss)
- **Rival moves**: A consort makes a play against you (ignoring = Slander gained)
- **Agent crises**: A follower is in trouble (ignoring = agent lost or condition worsened)
- **Opportunities**: A one-time chance (rare item, recruit, intelligence) that expires

### Multi-Day Events

Some events take 2-5 days to resolve, locking assigned agents:
- **Festival preparation**: 3-5 days of planning, rehearsal, execution
- **Investigation**: 2-3 days of gathering evidence, interviewing, confrontation
- **Pregnancy protection**: Ongoing daily assignment until birth
- **Building projects**: 3-7 days for estate improvements

## Contemplation at the Bronze Mirror (铜镜沉思)

> Sultan's Game equivalent: Methinks

Once per day, you gaze into your bronze mirror and contemplate a card. The result depends on what you ponder:

| Card Pondered | Result |
|---|---|
| **Silver taels** | Triggers 1 of 5 income events: silk trading, calligraphy commissions, medicine sale, tutoring, gambling den |
| **Intelligence scroll** | Triggers info-gathering: eavesdropping at the gardens, intercepting a letter, reading the stars |
| **Agent with mirror icon** | Advances that agent's personal storyline |
| **Chunhua's Resentment** | Spawns "Resolve Loyalty" event |
| **Equipment/Treasures** | Display for Ceremony edicts, or discover hidden properties |
| **Edict cards** | May reveal alternative fulfillment paths (e.g., a Favor edict can be broken through a Ceremony event if the right conditions are met) |
| **Reputation cards** | May trigger reputation-specific events (Virtue → temple vision, Ruthlessness → dark opportunity) |

## Court Politics — Morning Court (朝会)

> Sultan's Game equivalent: The Grand Game

Daily event when consorts gather (with or without the Emperor):

### Structure
1. **Attendance**: Assign noble-tagged agents to accompany you. More = stronger but more exposed.
2. **Support/Opposition**: Each NPC consort has a stance toward you. Build support through gifts, favors, poetry.
3. **Exchanges**: Political conversations where you can: praise, deflect, accuse, gift, recite poetry, remain silent.
4. **Slander**: Rivals whisper against you. Each whisper = 1 Slander card. You can counter-whisper but failure = doubled penalty.
5. **Emperor's Attention** (when present): Opportunity to gain Imperial Favor through performance, gifts, or wit.

### Slander Resolution
- 3 Slander cards accumulated → **Imperial Rebuke** event
- Imperial Rebuke: lose 5 Imperial Favor + punishment (demotion risk, agent confiscation, public humiliation)
- After rebuke, all 3 Slander cards destroyed
- Prevention: counter-slander events, bribing eunuchs to suppress rumors, building allied consort support

## Scandals — Palace Crises (宫闱丑闻)

Scandals are **distinct from Imperial Edicts**. Edicts are demands from the Emperor — they appear on schedule and must be broken within the timer. Scandals are eruptions: crises that break out without warning and demand immediate containment. They consume your days, agents, and resources even as you're trying to fulfill other obligations.

### How Scandals Work

- Scandals appear as timed crisis cards with **2–3 day deadlines**
- Each has a **Severity**: Clay / Bronze / Silver / Gold
- Ignored past deadline: the scandal escalates (gains 1 severity, generates Slander)
- Most scandals offer 3–4 resolution paths — the right one depends on your resources, stats, and moral priorities
- The **Scapegoat path** is always available if you have an eligible servant

---

### The Scapegoat Mechanic (替罪羊)

When a scandal strikes, you can offer a servant from your household as the guilty party — regardless of truth.

**Requirements:**
- You must have a **servant** (not a follower/ally — servants are assigned staff, not recruited characters) in your wing's roster
- The servant must be available (not Involved in another event)

**Effect:**
- Scandal resolves immediately, **no check required**
- The servant is: Disgraced (Clay), Imprisoned (Bronze), Flogged and Exiled (Silver), or Executed (Gold)
- The servant card is permanently removed from your deck
- Ruthlessness: +1 (Clay), +1 (Bronze), +2 (Silver), +3 (Gold)
- **Chunhua Resentment +1** if she witnesses or later discovers what happened

**Strategic tension**: Scapegoating is fast and reliable but depletes your servant pool and accumulates Resentment with Chunhua. Over-relying on it is a valid short-term strategy that creates fragility — and eventually a loyalty crisis.

---

### Scandal Type 1 — Defamation (毁誉之祸)

*A rival consort — or her agent — spreads lies about you. Rumors travel from servant to servant until they reach court ears.*

**Common triggers**: Losing a court exchange; having high Imperial Favour (rivals respond); as part of a rival's Dominion edict against you

**Severity range**: Clay to Silver

| Resolution Path | Requirements | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| **Contest at Court** | Eloquence + Cunning (vs. accuser's Eloquence) | Success: Slander removed, accuser embarrassed. Failure: Slander +1 |
| **Bribe the Eunuch Gossip** | 2–4 silver (based on severity) | Rumor suppressed silently; no reputation change |
| **Expose the Source** | Discretion + Cunning, 2-day event | Success: rival consort gains Disgrace. Ruthlessness +1 |
| **Scapegoat** | An eligible servant | Blame shifts entirely. Scandal resolved. Servant removed. |

---

### Scandal Type 2 — Improper Association (私交之丑)

*Rumors surface that you or someone in your household has been meeting inappropriately with a man — a guard, a scholar, a visiting official.*

**Common triggers**: Having a male agent assigned to your wing; Chunhua's subplot (Guard Chen); late-night location events

**Severity range**: Bronze to Gold (Gold = witnessed by a credible court figure)

| Resolution Path | Requirements | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| **Public Declaration of Loyalty** | Beauty + Eloquence at Morning Court | Success: Favour +1, Slander cleared. Must have Favour ≥ 11 to be believed |
| **Produce a Witness to Your Innocence** | Must have a Bronze+ Noble or Eunuch agent to vouch | Testimony accepted. The vouching agent gains Empress's attention (risk) |
| **Silence the Witness** | Discretion + Ruthlessness 2+ + Shadow Reach | Witness disappears. Shadow Reach +1. Ruthlessness +1 |
| **Scapegoat** | The accused male servant | He is removed and accused. If a follower (not servant), he may resist — contested Cunning check |

---

### Scandal Type 3 — Lost Valuables (失物之灾)

*Something of value has gone missing and your wing is implicated — a jade ornament, a gifted robe, an imperial letter.*

**Common triggers**: Rival's Dominion edict targeting your resources; certain Bronze palace events; random

**Severity range**: Clay to Silver

| Resolution Path | Requirements | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| **Investigate and Return** | Cunning + Discretion, 2-day event | Find the real culprit. Virtue +1. May expose a rival's agent in the process |
| **Replace the Item** | 2–6 silver based on item value | Problem quietly resolved. No reputation change |
| **Claim it was a Gift** | Eloquence check | Reframe the situation — you gave it deliberately. Success: resolved. Failure: Slander +1 |
| **Scapegoat** | An eligible servant | Servant accused of theft. Scandal resolved. |

---

### Scandal Type 4 — Protocol Breach (失礼之罪)

*You or someone in your household committed a visible breach of palace protocol — speaking out of turn, entering a restricted space, wearing improper attire at a ceremony.*

**Common triggers**: Failed court checks; hasty decisions; certain restricted-location events

**Severity range**: Clay to Bronze (rarely escalates higher on its own)

| Resolution Path | Requirements | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| **Public Apology** | Resolve check — accept blame with dignity | Imperial Favour −1, Slander removed, Virtue +1. The Empress Dowager notices your composure |
| **Prove Protocol Was Followed** | Scholarship + evidence (requires a recent Imperial Library visit) | Technically correct argument. Accepted if Favour ≥ 11 |
| **Bribe the Record Keeper** | 1–3 silver | Incident omitted from court records |
| **Scapegoat** | An eligible servant | Attributed to unauthorized servant behavior |

---

### Scandal Type 5 — Witchcraft Accusation (巫蛊之祸)

*The most dangerous scandal. Someone claims to have found evidence of forbidden arts in your possession — a cursed effigy, a poison charm, forbidden sutras near your bed.*

Wu Zetian herself faced this. It is the weapon of last resort for an enemy who has nothing left to lose — or everything to gain.

**Common triggers**: High Ruthlessness (6+); certain Spiritual Arts events; Gold-tier Empress scheme; rare random event

**Severity range**: Silver to Gold — always serious, never Clay or Bronze

| Resolution Path | Requirements | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| **Temple Purification Ritual** | Spiritual Arts check + Virtue 3+, 2-day event, 3 silver | Public display of piety. Success: charges dismissed, Virtue +2. Failure: Slander +2 |
| **Investigate to Find Who Planted It** | Cunning + Discretion, 3-day event | Expose the conspirator. Massive reward but risky — if you fail mid-investigation, Slander +3 |
| **Imperial Favour Shield** | Requires Favour ≥ 31 (Cherished band) | Emperor dismisses charges without requiring your defense |
| **Scapegoat** | An eligible servant — accused of practicing dark arts in your wing | Servant Executed. Scandal resolved. Ruthlessness +3. Chunhua Resentment +2 |

---

### Scandal Type 6 — Financial Corruption (贪墨之嫌)

*Records show irregularities in your household accounts — inflated expenses, missing silver, suspected bribery received.*

**Common triggers**: High Shadow Reach activity (6+); certain Dominion events; rival's Justice-type edict targeting you

**Severity range**: Clay to Silver

| Resolution Path | Requirements | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| **Make the Accounts Whole** | Pay 3–8 silver (must have it available) | Problem disappears quietly |
| **Present Clean Records** | Scholarship + Cunning (requires recent Library visit for record-keeping) | Discrepancy explained away legally |
| **Counter-Bribe the Investigators** | Shadow Reach 3+ and 3 silver | Investigation quietly buried. Shadow Reach +1 |
| **Scapegoat Your Household Manager** | Eligible servant in manager role | They take the blame and are arrested. Your Estate Management income drops by 1 silver/day until you assign a replacement |

---

### Scandal Escalation

If a scandal is ignored past its deadline:

| Current Severity | After Deadline | Additional Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Clay | → Bronze | Slander +1 |
| Bronze | → Silver | Slander +1 + Imperial Favour −1 |
| Silver | → Gold | Slander +2 + Imperial Favour −2 + Imperial Rebuke possible |
| Gold | → Crisis | Immediate Imperial Rebuke + rank demotion event triggered |

## Event Branching

Most events are not binary pass/fail. They branch based on:

1. **Which agents you assign** — different agents unlock different approach options
2. **Which optional slots you fill** — bonus agents open alternate paths
3. **Check results** — degrees of success matter
4. **Your reputation state** — high Virtue vs. high Ruthlessness unlocks different choices
5. **Active conditions** — a pregnant, poisoned, or disgraced protagonist changes what's available

### Example: "The Missing Jade Bracelet"

A Bronze Justice edict. The Empress's jade bracelet is missing. Suspicion falls on your wing.

**Path A — Investigate** (Cunning + Discretion): Find the real thief. Success → Virtue +1, Favour +1. Failure → Slander +1.

**Path B — Frame a Rival** (Discretion + Cunning; requires Ruthlessness 3): Plant evidence on a competing consort. Success → rival disgraced. Failure → you're exposed, Slander +2.

**Path C — Buy Your Way Out** (3 silver + Eloquence): Bribe the investigating eunuch. Scandal resolved quietly. No reputation change.

**Path D — Accept Blame with Dignity** (Resolve check): You take responsibility. Lose 1 Imperial Favour, gain Virtue +2. The Empress Dowager notices.

**Path E — Scapegoat** (any eligible servant): Blame shifts immediately. No check. Servant Imprisoned. Ruthlessness +1.
