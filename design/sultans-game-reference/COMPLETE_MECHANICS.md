# The Sultan's Game — Complete Mechanical Reference

> This is a reference document. It captures how The Sultan's Game works mechanically so we can use it as a foundation for Inner Officials. This file is not meant to be edited over time — it's a snapshot.

## Core Loop

A run spans a deck of **28 Sultan Cards**. Each card starts a **7-day cycle** — break the card within 7 days or die. Breaking a card forces the next draw immediately.

**Daily Turn:**
1. Events appear on the map (some timed with 1-3 day deadlines)
2. Assign character cards to event slots (red = mandatory, white = optional bonus)
3. Use **Methinks** once per day (drag a card onto a skeleton figure to trigger special events based on card type)
4. End day — all assigned events resolve via dice checks
5. Repeat until Sultan Card is broken or timer expires

**Strategic nuance:** You can hold a completed card-breaking event without resolving it, postponing the forced draw and buying time to accumulate resources.

## Agent / Character System

Characters are literal cards you drag onto event slots — the core "worker" unit.

### 9 Stats
- Physique, Charisma, Wisdom, Stealth, Combat, Sociability, Survival, Magic, Knowledge

### Card Tiers (power level)
| Tier | Description |
|---|---|
| **Stone** | Commoners, slaves, lowest rank |
| **Bronze** | Talented individuals |
| **Silver** | Remarkable people (the protagonist is Silver) |
| **Gold** | Royals, exceptional figures |

Tier matters because Sultan Cards require breaking acts of matching or higher tier.

### Tags
- **Ownership**: "Owned" (items) vs. "Follower" (autonomous characters)
- **Status**: Noble, Slave, Free Person, Vizier
- **Equipment slots**: Weapon, Attire, Accessory
- **Conditions**: Injured, Cursed, Sick, Depressed, Frozen, Poisoned, Knocked Out
- **Combat roles**: Troop, Commander, Messenger, Standard-Bearer
- **Economy**: Value, Hirable, Stackable

### Stat Improvement
- **Books**: Purchased at the Bookstore for 1 gold; permanently boost a stat
- **Equipment**: Weapons, attire, accessories providing stat bonuses (stacking additively, up to +5)
- **Troops**: Trained at Riel's Gang for 5 gold (e.g., Mud Crawlers, Gun Maidens)
- **Character storylines**: Progressing a character's personal narrative can upgrade their tier

### Recruitment
- Story events and quests
- **Guesthouse** (unlocked at Notoriety 3, costs 10 gold): offers 3 retainers, refreshes every 7 days
- **Retainers** are "one-use equipment" rather than full characters
- **Fate's Ledger boons** can add specific characters to starting hand

### Losing Characters
- Death in events, duels, or assassinations
- Sultan confiscation (e.g., takes a beautiful female follower for 7 days, returns Injured)
- Characters become "Involved" (locked) while participating in multi-day events
- Conditions take characters out of commission until healed

### Wife (Maggie) — Special Mechanics
- Strongest starting follower with unique event chains
- **Resentment mechanic**: stacks if neglected. Festers too long → she murders you ("The Eternal Dream" ending)
- Pondering the Resentment card with Methinks spawns the "Resolve Wife's Resentment" ritual

## Resource System

### Primary Currency: Gold Coins
- **Generated by**: Estate management (daily, Wisdom + Sociability check), Methinks pondering of gold coins (triggers 1 of 5 random money-making rituals), event rewards, Jackal Alliance passive income (based on Notoriety, every 7 days)
- **Spent on**: Books (1 gold), healing (1 gold), Guesthouse building (10 gold), troop training (5 gold), equipment, Sultan Card event costs, court gifts, bribes

### Intelligence Cards
- Consumable information cards earned from Grand Game wins, Bathhouse, Methinks pondering, events
- **Used as rerolls** during dice checks — critical safety net

### Golden Dice
- Extremely rare. Provide automatic successes (no roll needed)
- Sources: temple blessings, rare events. Starting count depends on difficulty

### 5 Reputation Metrics

| Metric | How Gained | Key Thresholds |
|---|---|---|
| **Renown** | Virtuous acts, restraint | 3: Mysterious Assistance (trade 1g→10g, upgrade stats). 10: Reset Sultan Card timer 3x. 20: Break any card free |
| **Infamy** | Evil deeds, abuse | 3: Challengers appear. 5: Dark opportunities. 10: Assassination plots. 20: Evil Incarnate |
| **Influence** | Court politics, gifts to Sultan | General political power. +1 for avoiding Slander 6 consecutive Grand Games. -5 when 3 Slanders accumulate |
| **Notoriety** | Outlaw/freemen rep | 3: Guesthouse unlock. 5: Criminal retainers. Affects Jackal Alliance income |
| **Insight** | Supernatural sensitivity | Progressive Mind Battle stages (up to 28). Unlocks supernatural encounters |

### Special State Cards
- **Slander**: Accumulates from court. At 3: lose 5 Influence + Sultan's Mockery event. Then destroyed.
- **Wife's Resentment**: Stacking negative. Must resolve or risk assassination.
- **Convictions**: Immaculate Piety (upgradeable), Heretical God (cult path), Traditional/Reformative balance

## Event System

Events (called **Rituals**) are the primary interaction. Drag character cards into event slots on the map.

### Slot Types
- **Red slots**: Mandatory — all must be filled to begin
- **White slots**: Optional — bonus checks or choices
- **Involved**: Characters locked until event resolves

### Methinks Pondering (once per day)
Drag a card onto the skeleton figure:
- **Gold Coins** → 1 of 5 random money-making rituals
- **Intelligence** → Performing at the Feast or Royal Library
- **Characters with skeleton icon** → advance personal storyline
- **Specific characters**: Hassan/Jenna→craft items; Malkina→equip; Arzuna→feasts; Habib→cooks
- **Wife's Resentment** → Resolve Wife's Resentment ritual
- **Treasures** → build display structures (gold cost), can count as Extravagance
- **Sultan Cards** → may reveal breaking methods
- **Convictions** → Piety upgrade or cult events

### Key Recurring Events
| Event | Function |
|---|---|
| The Grand Game | Court politics, Influence, Intelligence gathering |
| Managing the Estate | Daily gold (Wisdom + Sociability) |
| News in the Bathhouse | Intelligence gathering (Noble only) |
| Bookstore Business | Buy books, befriend nobles |
| House of Delights | Break Carnality cards (protagonist only) |
| Glimpse of God | Mind Battle progression (Insight threshold) |

## Dice Resolution

1. Count relevant stat total from all assigned character cards
2. Roll that many dice
3. Each die: binary success/fail (60% success on Easy, lower on harder)
4. Event specifies required successes

### Modifiers
- **Contested checks**: enemy stats subtract from your pool. If enemy ≥ yours, automatic failure
- **Rerolls** (from Intelligence cards): re-roll all dice in the check
- **Golden Dice**: add automatic successes without rolling

## The Court ("Grand Game")

Daily event when Sultan is present:
- Noble characters attend
- Each noble has Support/Opposition stance toward you
- Build support through gifts, favors, alliances
- **Slander** accumulates from rivals whispering against you
- 3 Slander = lose 5 Influence + punishment event
- You can counter, but failure doubles the penalty

## Sultan Card System

28 cards across **4 tiers** (Stone/Bronze/Silver/Gold) and **4 types**:

| Type | Theme |
|---|---|
| **Bloodshed** | Combat, violence, duels |
| **Carnality** | Seduction, passion, romance |
| **Conquest** | Military, territorial control |
| **Extravagance** | Wealth display, spectacle |

Each requires specific events and character tiers to break. Higher tiers are harder — Gold Conquest is described as "the hardest card to break in the game" and can softlock a run.

## Map / Locations

Presented as a worn cloth/rug. Locations are nodes with event titles, descriptions, timers, and card slots.

**Always available**: Home, The Court, Estate, Bathhouse, Bookstore, House of Delights

**Unlockable**:
- Guesthouse (Notoriety 3 + 10 gold)
- Sanitarium (when a follower is injured)
- Nursery (via questlines)
- Riel's Gang (via quest — troop training)
- Jackal Alliance (alternative to Riel's Gang — passive income)
- Treasure displays (built via Methinks + gold)

## Meta-Progression

### 1001 Nights (Achievements)
- Award **Fate Points** (1 per story completed)
- Categories: progression milestones, card-breaking, endings, relationships, thresholds, survival duration
- Count across all runs

### Fate's Ledger (Permanent Upgrades)
Spend Fate Points on boons activated at run start:

| Category | Cost | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Stat Boons | 3-5 FP | +2 to a protagonist stat |
| Starting Characters | 20 FP | Add specific character to starting hand |
| Follower Upgrades | 8-20 FP | Upgrade wife/follower tier and stats |
| Equipment Cards | 10-20 FP | Lucky Amulet, Bath VIP Card, Book Rental Club |
| Resource Cards | 2 FP | +5 Gold Coins (stackable) |
| Sultan Card Pool | 10 FP | Add 4 extra cards of a tier (dilutes hard cards) |
| Status Cards | 20 FP | Boost a reputation metric via Methinks |
| Advanced Boons | 30-40 FP | Extra exchanges, rewinds, twin character, hidden stories |

## Difficulty (Storyteller System)

| Setting | Die Success Rate | Rewinds | Exchanges | Golden Dice |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Easy (Maggie) | 60% | Unlimited | Yes | Most |
| Normal (Hassan) | ~50% | Limited | Limited | Moderate |
| Hard (The Magician) | ~40% | Very limited | Very limited | Fewest |

### Within-Run Scaling
- Easier Stone/Bronze cards depleted early → harder Silver/Gold remain
- Resources (Intelligence, gold, golden dice) consumed over time
- Character attrition from injuries, death, confiscation
- Reputation consequences escalate (Infamy → assassinations, Slander → demotion)

## Win/Loss Conditions

### Loss (Death)
- Fail to break a Sultan Card in 7 days (most common)
- Wife murders you (unresolved resentment)
- Die in duel, assassination, Sultan's wrath
- Various story-specific deaths

### Victory (50+ endings)
1. **Countless Times** — Break all 28 Sultan Cards (standard win)
2. **The Collector** — Break all 28 in under 14 days
3. **New Banner** — Revolution (15+ sub-endings)
4. **True Liberation** — Slay the Magician
5. **Distant Paradise** — Escape the city
6. **Descent of the Gods** — Summon God
7. **Dragonslayer** — Slay a dragon
8. **Invitation from the Abyss** — Hidden evil ending (20 Infamy)

## Card Visual Design (Reference for Inner Officials)

This section documents the Sultan's Game card visual language specifically for use when designing Inner Officials cards.

### Card Face Layout

```
┌─────────────────────────┐
│  [TIER ORNAMENTAL FRAME] │  ← Frame colour/style is the tier signal
│  ┌───────────────────┐  │
│  │                   │  │
│  │   PORTRAIT ART    │  │  ← Fills ~65% of card face
│  │  (upgrades with   │  │
│  │   tier promotion) │  │
│  └───────────────────┘  │
│  NAME          [TIER]   │  ← Name strip; tier label top-right
│  ──────────────────────  │
│  ⚔3  💃5  📚2  🌿1     │  ← Stat icons + numbers (only non-zero shown)
│  [Wpn][Att][Acc]         │  ← Equipment slot icons
│  [Noble][Female][Dancer] │  ← Classification tags
└─────────────────────────┘
```

### Tier Frame Design
Each tier has a distinct ornamental frame — the border is the primary tier signal, not a badge:
- **Stone**: Rough unfinished stone border, grey-brown
- **Bronze**: Weathered copper filigree with archaic vessel (taotie-like) motifs  
- **Silver**: Clean silver lattice with refined geometric patterning
- **Gold**: Ornate gold flourishes with crowns/gem accents

Frame complexity increases with tier. A Gold card is visually heavier and more elaborate than a Stone card.

### Portrait Treatment
- Portrait art occupies the majority of the card face
- Live2D animated in Sultan's Game (static in our game initially)
- The portrait itself upgrades visually when a character's tier is promoted (the same character appears in finer clothing, with more elaborate styling)
- For agents without custom art: a **stylised silhouette in the same frame** is used — same border, same colour treatment, shadowed figure in a thematically appropriate pose

### Stats on Card Face
- Only stats above 0 are shown
- Each stat has a **small icon symbol** (not text), with a number beside it
- Stats are arranged horizontally in a compact row — typically 4-5 stats visible at once
- Stats visually update when conditions apply penalties (shown as red/reduced numbers)

### Equipment Slots
- Shown as **3 small square slots** with category icons: Weapon · Attire · Accessory
- Empty slots show a faded icon; filled slots show the item's icon or coloured fill
- Items are cards themselves — dragged from hand onto slots

### Classification Tags
- Displayed as small coloured chips along the card bottom
- Examples: Noble, Slave, Female, Dancer, Troop, Follower, Free Person
- Tags gate event eligibility (e.g. "requires Noble") — they are mechanical, not merely flavour

### Condition Display
- Active conditions appear as small icons overlaid on the portrait corner
- The underlying stat numbers visibly drop to reflect penalties
- Severely blocked characters (Frozen, Imprisoned) get a full overlay tint

### Hand Display
- Characters are held in a **scrollable hand at the bottom** of the screen
- Cards are dragged from hand to event slots on the map
- Cards in use ("Involved") are locked with a visual indicator until the event resolves
- The hand also contains item cards, intelligence cards, and Sultan Cards — all unified as a card hand
