### B. Event Categories & Historical Source Material

#### B1. Court Politics (Morning Court / Inner Court)

| Event Seed | Historical Basis | Game Mechanics |
|---|---|---|
| **The Elevation Debate** | 655: Gaozong proposes making Wu empress. Zhangsun Wuji and Chu Suiliang oppose. Li Yifu supports. | Multi-day crisis. Build support among officials. Dilemma: bribe, threaten, or persuade. |
| **The Accusation of Witchcraft** | Empress Wang and her mother accused of practicing witchcraft (gu poison, 蛊毒) against Wu's newborn | Scandal event. Plant evidence or discover real plot. Reputation consequences. |
| **The Draft Abdication** | 664: Shangguan Yi convinces Gaozong to draft an edict removing Wu from power. Wu discovers it. | Crisis event. Intercept the document. Dilemma: mercy or destroy Shangguan. |
| **Slander at the Grand Audience** | Regular occurrence: rival concubines and officials speak against you | Recurring event. Counter with eloquence, intelligence scrolls, or allies. |
| **The Regency Question** | Gaozong's health declines. Who rules when he cannot? | Late-game political arc. Build case for "curtain governance" (垂帘听政). |
| **Purge of the Old Guard** | 659: Zhangsun Wuji and allies systematically removed | Major storyline. Each purge is an event with moral weight. |

#### B2. Harem Intrigue (Your Chambers / Imperial Gardens)

| Event Seed | Historical Basis | Game Mechanics |
|---|---|---|
| **The Dead Princess** | Wu's infant daughter found dead; Empress Wang had just visited. Wu may have killed her own child to frame Wang. | Defining storyline. Dilemma: did you do it? Choice affects entire run. |
| **The Human Pig** | After deposing Wang and Xiao, Wu allegedly had their hands and feet cut off and placed in wine jars | Late event. Ruthlessness vs. Virtue defining moment. |
| **The Emperor's Wandering Eye** | Gaozong takes interest in other women (historically, Xiao Liangdi) | Recurring tension. Dilemma: seduce, scheme, or accept. |
| **Pregnancy & Heirs** | Wu bore 4 sons and 1 daughter. Pregnancy = power but also vulnerability. | Personal events. Agent (you) locked for duration. Strategic timing. |
| **The Rival's Poison** | Various poisoning attempts (real or fabricated) were constant | Investigation/personal events. Echoes your Poisoned Hairpin storyline. |
| **Festival of the Weaver** (Qixi) | 7th night of 7th month. Romantic festival. | Seasonal event. Romance/favor opportunities. |
| **Cold Palace** | Fallen concubines confined to neglected quarters | Consequence location. Being sent here = near-death state. |

#### B3. Religion & Spirituality (Buddhist Temple / Chambers)

| Event Seed | Historical Basis | Game Mechanics |
|---|---|---|
| **The Maitreya Prophecy** | Wu used a forged Buddhist text (大云经 Great Cloud Sutra) claiming a female bodhisattva would rule | Major storyline. Commission the forgery, spread it, use it for legitimacy. |
| **The Famen Temple Relic** | Finger bone of the Buddha, periodically brought to court | Spiritual event. Devotion vs political calculation. |
| **Buddhist vs Taoist Debate** | Gaozong initially favored Taoism (Li family claimed descent from Laozi). Wu promoted Buddhism for political differentiation. | Recurring faction tension. Choose which to patronize. |
| **The White Horse Temple** | Wu's favorite monk Xue Huaiyi was made abbot | Ally/location event. Dangerous alliance. |
| **The Burning of the Mingtang** | 695: Xue Huaiyi, jealous of the Zhang brothers, burned Wu's grand ceremonial hall | Late-game crisis. Betrayal from within. |
| **Ancestor Worship Ceremonies** | Regular rites at imperial ancestral temple | Ceremony events. Protocol precision required. |
| **The Vegetarian Decree** | Wu periodically banned meat/alcohol for Buddhist reasons | Policy event with economic/political consequences. |

#### B4. Scandals & Crises

| Event Seed | Historical Basis | Game Mechanics |
|---|---|---|

| **The Classic of Entrapment** | Lai Junchen's actual torture manual, institutionalized false confessions | Item/mechanic. Ruthlessness tool. |
| **Anonymous Denunciation Boxes** | Wu installed bronze boxes (铜匦) for anyone to submit accusations | Mechanic: spend intelligence scrolls to anonymously accuse. |

| **Tomb Desecration** | Zhangsun Wuji's tomb opened, family exiled | Consequence of purge events. Ruthlessness milestone. |

### B8. Court Rituals & Daily Life

| Event Seed | Historical Basis | Game Mechanics |
|---|---|---|
| **The Jade Tablet** | Emperor's bedchamber companion selected nightly by flipping name tablets on a tray. Chosen consort carried to his chambers wrapped in a quilt by eunuchs. | Recurring Favor event. Being chosen = Favor boost + pregnancy chance. Not chosen repeatedly = rivals gain ground. |
| **Morning Obeisance** | All consorts assembled daily to bow to the Empress in rank order. Hierarchy enforced through ritual humiliation. | Daily passive event. Wrong protocol = Slander. Correct protocol = nothing (that's the point). |
| **The Cold Food Festival** | 3 days before Qingming: no fires lit. Everyone eats cold food. Palace goes dark. | Atmospheric event. Vulnerability window — no kitchen, guards less alert. Opportunity for schemes. |
| **The Turning of Seasons** | Imperial astronomers announced season changes. Getting the calendar wrong was a cosmic failure. | Ceremony event. Scholarship check to advise correctly. |
| **The Imperial Hunt** | Autumn hunts in palace grounds. Men displayed martial prowess. Women watched and judged. | Seasonal event. Your guards' Martial stat matters. Impress the Emperor with your household's strength. |

### B9. Key Specific Incidents (Game-Ready)

| Event Seed | Historical Detail | Game Translation |
|---|---|---|
| **"Please Enter the Urn"** | Lai Junchen asked Zhou Xing "how to make a prisoner confess?" Zhou described heating a large urn. Lai said "please get in." Origin of the Chinese idiom. | Late-game dilemma within Secret Police storyline. Turn your own tools against each other. |
| **The Lion Stallion** | Young Wu told Emperor Taizong she could tame his unbreakable horse with "an iron whip, an iron mace, and a dagger." Taizong was impressed and unsettled. | Backstory reference. Defines protagonist's character. Could be a flashback event or reputation gate flavor text. |
| **The Cat Ban** | After Consort Xiao cursed "I'll be reborn as a cat to tear out Wu's throat," Wu banned all cats from the palace. | Atmospheric detail + paranoia mechanic. After Xiao's downfall, cats trigger Heavenly Sight events. |
| **The Surname Change** | Wu changed Empress Wang's surname to "Python" (蟒) and Xiao's to "Owl" (枭) — evil animals. | Ruthlessness consequence. Degrading your enemies in the official record. |
| **The Move to Luoyang** | After the wine jar executions, Wu was haunted by nightmares. She moved the entire court to Luoyang and rarely returned to Chang'an. | Consequence of extreme ruthlessness choices. Location change mid-game. |
| **Shangguan Wan'er's Origin** | Granddaughter of the chancellor Wu had executed. Raised as a slave in the palace. Wu recognized her talent and made her personal secretary. | NPC recruitment event. The most complex loyalty relationship in the game — serving the woman who killed your grandfather. |
| **The Wordless Stele** | Wu's tombstone at Qianling Mausoleum is famously blank. She requested no inscription — let history judge. | Ending flavor. The "balanced reputation" ending. |
| **Wu's Invented Characters** | Wu created 12 new Chinese characters, including one for her own name combining "sky" and "space." | Late-game legitimacy mechanic. Scholarship + Heavenly Sight event. |
| **The Vairocana Buddha** | The giant Buddha at Longmen Grottoes is said to be carved in Wu's likeness. | Buddhist doctrine capstone event. Build a monument to yourself as divine. |

### D. Doctrines (Conviction Paths)

| Doctrine | Chinese | Theme | Gameplay Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Buddhist Devotion** | 佛道 | Compassion, karma, the Maitreya prophecy | Heavenly Sight ↑, Virtue ↑. Unlocks temple events, spiritual allies, legitimacy through prophecy. |
| **Taoist Cultivation** | 道修 | Immortality, alchemy, harmony with nature | Heavenly Sight ↑, Vitality ↑. Unlocks elixir events, health benefits, but politically neutral. |
| **Confucian Propriety** | 儒礼 | Order, hierarchy, filial piety, scholarship | Virtue ↑, Scholarship ↑. Strengthens court legitimacy but constrains ruthless actions. |
| **Legalist Ruthlessness** | 法术 | Control through law, punishment, surveillance | Ruthlessness ↑, Shadow Reach ↑. Unlocks secret police, denunciation boxes, purge events. |

Each doctrine is upgradeable (Clay → Jade tier). Higher tiers unlock more powerful events and choices within dilemmas.

### G. Political Structures (Game Reference)

| Institution | Chinese | Role | Game Relevance |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Secretariat** | 中书省 | Drafts edicts | Where imperial edicts originate. Your edicts must pass through here. |
| **Chancellery** | 门下省 | Reviews/vetoes edicts | Can block your plans. Need allies here. |
| **Dept. of State Affairs** | 尚书省 | Executes policy | The bureaucratic machine. Controls the Six Ministries. |
| **Censorate** | 御史台 | Impeachment, surveillance | Can be weaponized against rivals OR against you. |
| **Palace Domestic Service** | 内侍省 | Eunuch administration | Your intelligence network inside the palace. |
| **Imperial Guard** | 千牛卫 | Palace security | Military control = coup prevention/capability. |

**Key dynamic:** Inner court (harem, eunuchs) vs. outer court (officials, chancellors). Wu's genius was eroding this boundary through "curtain governance" — sitting behind a screen at audiences, officially "assisting" but actually ruling.
