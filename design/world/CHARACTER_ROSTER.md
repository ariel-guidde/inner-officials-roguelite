# ACT 1: TAIZONG'S COURT

## The Emperor

**Emperor Taizong** (太宗 李世民) — Jade tier
- Age: Late 40s, declining
- Tags: male, imperial
- Stats: Beauty 2, Cunning 5, Eloquence 4, Discretion 3, Resolve 5, Vitality 3→declining, Resourcefulness 4, Scholarship 4, Spiritual Arts 2
- **Personality:** The greatest emperor in Chinese history. Founded the dynasty by killing his own brothers at Xuanwu Gate. Brilliant general, shrewd politician, patron of arts. He doesn't suffer fools. He barely notices low-ranking concubines — getting his attention is genuinely hard. He respects boldness and intelligence over beauty.
- **Health:** Obsessed with Taoist longevity elixirs (which are slowly poisoning him). His health declines over Act 1. Player can observe, help (physician events), or exploit this. Death day is semi-random (30–40).
- **Romance path:** Possible but hardest in the game. The Lion Stallion anecdote: he asked how to tame an unbreakable horse, Wu said "an iron whip, an iron mace, and a dagger." He was impressed and unsettled.
- **Game role:** Source of edicts. Distant, imposing presence. His favor is the ultimate prize in Act 1.

## The Empress (Act 1)

**Empress Zhangsun** (长孙皇后) — DECEASED before game start
- Taizong's beloved wife. Died 636, three years before Wu entered the palace.
- Her absence matters: there is no empress. The inner court lacks a central authority. This creates a power vacuum among the consorts.
- Her brother Zhangsun Wuji is the most powerful official at court — he carries her legacy and protects her sons.

**Note:** The Empress position is EMPTY in Act 1. This is a key dynamic — the consorts compete without a clear hierarchy at the top.

## The Three Princes

### Prince Chengqian (李承乾) — "The Crown Prince"
- Title: Crown Prince (太子)
- Tier: Gold | Age: Mid 20s
- Tags: male, imperial
- Stats: Beauty 3, Cunning 3, Eloquence 3, Discretion 1, Resolve 2, Vitality 2, Resourcefulness 2, Scholarship 3, Spiritual Arts 1
- **Personality:** Tormented heir. Expected to be his legendary father's copy and knows he can't. Rebellious — fascinated by Turkic culture, hunting, reckless behavior. Has a male lover, the musician Cheng Xinnu. The court whispers. His father is in denial.
- **What he values:** Loyalty, acceptance, someone who sees him as a person not a title.
- **Difficulty:** Easy entry (he's the heir, backing him is natural). Challenge is keeping him from self-destructing.
- **Storyline hooks:** His relationship with Xinnu is a time bomb. Help him manage it, or watch it explode. If he becomes emperor: unconventional court, you're a political advisor not a consort.

**Cheng Xinnu** (称心) — Bronze tier
- Tags: male, commoner, entertainer
- Stats: Beauty 5, Cunning 1, Eloquence 3, Discretion 1, Resolve 1, Vitality 2, Scholarship 1
- **Personality:** A musician. Beautiful, gentle, completely out of his depth in palace politics. He loves Chengqian genuinely. He's not scheming — he's just a person who fell in love with the wrong person.
- **Storyline hooks:** Protecting him protects Chengqian's stability. If Taizong discovers and executes him (historically accurate), Chengqian snaps.

### Prince Tai (李泰) — "The Scholar Prince"
- Title: Prince of Wei (魏王)
- Tier: Gold | Age: Early 20s
- Tags: male, imperial
- Stats: Beauty 2, Cunning 5, Eloquence 5, Discretion 4, Resolve 3, Vitality 2, Resourcefulness 3, Scholarship 5, Spiritual Arts 2
- **Personality:** Brilliant and dangerous. Compiled the Kuodi Zhi encyclopedia. His father's intellectual favorite. Beneath the scholarly surface: cold, calculating ambition. He flatters, gifts, impresses — and fabricates evidence against his brother.
- **What he values:** Intelligence, usefulness. He'll pursue YOU if your Scholarship and Cunning are high.
- **Difficulty:** Easy entry (he recruits useful people). Challenge is Act 2: surviving his paranoia.
- **Storyline hooks:** Alliance of schemers. Promises he'll never keep. If he becomes emperor: meritocratic but paranoid court with an eroding "Trust" meter.

### Prince Zhi (李治) — "The Gentle Prince"
- Title: Prince of Jin (晋王), ninth son
- Tier: Silver | Age: Late teens / ~20
- Tags: male, imperial
- Stats: Beauty 3, Cunning 2, Eloquence 4, Discretion 2, Resolve 1, Vitality 2, Resourcefulness 2, Scholarship 4, Spiritual Arts 3
- **Personality:** Gentle, romantic, kind. Loves poetry, Buddhist philosophy, quiet conversation. Not built for power. Falls in love easily and deeply. Drawn to strong women because he knows he's weak. The one who will visit you in the nunnery.
- **What he values:** Beauty, emotional connection, genuine kindness, shared interests (poetry, philosophy).
- **Difficulty:** HARD. Ninth son, no claim, overlooked. You have to seek him out (library, temple, poetry circles). Then engineer both brothers' disqualification. Then convince Taizong that Zhi is the safe choice. All as a Rank 9 nobody.
- **Storyline hooks:** The canon path. The richest Act 2 content. If he becomes emperor (Gaozong): you become the power behind the throne. His health declines. You rule.

## Taizong's Consorts (Act 1 Inner Court)

The Empress is dead. The inner court is a contest without a referee.

### Historical

**Consort Yang** (杨贵妃) — Gold tier, Rank 2 Guifei
- Tags: female, noble, concubine
- Stats: Beauty 4, Cunning 4, Eloquence 4, Discretion 3, Resolve 3, Vitality 3, Resourcefulness 3, Scholarship 3, Spiritual Arts 2
- **Personality:** The highest-ranking consort since the Empress's death. She's run the inner court for years. Politically savvy, maternal toward the younger princes (especially Zhi — she's his unofficial guardian). Not cruel, but protective of her position. She tolerates new concubines as long as they know their place.
- **Historical basis:** Consort Yang was a real historical figure, mother of some of Taizong's children and a senior presence in the inner court.
- **Storyline hooks:** The gatekeeper. Offend her and you're frozen out. Win her over and she's a powerful patron. She has complicated feelings about Chengqian (worried about him) and Tai (doesn't trust him).

**Consort Xu** (徐贤妃) — Silver tier, Rank 4
- Tags: female, noble, concubine
- Stats: Beauty 3, Cunning 3, Eloquence 5, Discretion 3, Resolve 3, Vitality 2, Resourcefulness 2, Scholarship 5, Spiritual Arts 2
- **Personality:** A genuine intellectual. Famous for her literary talent — she wrote memorials advising Taizong on governance (one of the few concubines to do so). She's the only woman Taizong respects as a mind, not just a beauty. She's also quietly dying of tuberculosis.
- **Historical basis:** Xu Hui (徐惠) was a real figure, known as the "scholar consort." She died of grief shortly after Taizong's death, refusing medicine.
- **Storyline hooks:** Potential mentor for Scholarship path. She sees your potential. She's also your rival for Taizong's intellectual attention. Her illness creates a ticking clock for her storyline.

### Fictional Consorts (filling ranks)

**Lady Jiang** (蒋修仪) — Silver tier, Rank 5
- Tags: female, noble, concubine
- Stats: Beauty 4, Cunning 3, Eloquence 2, Discretion 4, Resolve 2, Vitality 3, Resourcefulness 2, Scholarship 2, Spiritual Arts 1
- **Personality:** Beautiful, quiet, dangerous. She's maintained her position for a decade by knowing everyone's secrets and never revealing her own. She's not ambitious — she's a survivor. She has sons by Taizong, which makes her secure but also a target if succession gets ugly.
- **Storyline hooks:** Information broker. She'll trade secrets for protection. She knows things about the princes that could change everything — but she won't share for free.

**Lady Zheng** (郑充媛) — Silver tier, Rank 6
- Tags: female, noble, concubine
- Stats: Beauty 3, Cunning 5, Eloquence 4, Discretion 3, Resolve 2, Vitality 2, Resourcefulness 3, Scholarship 3, Spiritual Arts 1
- **Personality:** The schemer. She backed the wrong prince years ago and has been treading water since. She's bitter, sharp, and looking for a new horse to ride. She'll approach you if you show promise — she wants to be a kingmaker.
- **Storyline hooks:** Useful but unreliable ally. She'll switch sides if you weaken. She has access to outer-court gossip through a brother who's a minor official.

**Lady Song** (宋婕妤) — Bronze tier, Rank 7
- Tags: female, commoner, concubine
- Stats: Beauty 5, Cunning 1, Eloquence 2, Discretion 1, Resolve 2, Vitality 3, Resourcefulness 1, Scholarship 1, Spiritual Arts 2
- **Personality:** Young, stunning, devout. She entered the palace around the same time as you. She's terrified. She prays constantly. She has no political instincts whatsoever. She's the person the palace will eat alive.
- **Storyline hooks:** Protect her (Virtue path) or use her as a decoy. If she catches the Emperor's eye, she becomes an accidental rival. She has genuine spiritual sensitivity (Heavenly Sight events).

**Lady Pei** (裴美人) — Bronze tier, Rank 8
- Tags: female, noble, concubine
- Stats: Beauty 3, Cunning 2, Eloquence 3, Discretion 2, Resolve 3, Vitality 2, Resourcefulness 2, Scholarship 2, Spiritual Arts 1
- **Personality:** Practical, dry-humored, resigned. She's been Rank 8 for five years and has accepted her lot. She doesn't scheme, doesn't dream, just survives. She's the closest thing to a friend you'll find — because she wants nothing from you.
- **Storyline hooks:** Genuine friendship. No hidden agenda. She's the person you talk to when you need to be human. In the nunnery, she'll be there too.

## The Outer Court (Act 1)

### Historical Officials

**Zhangsun Wuji** (长孙无忌) — Gold tier
- Tags: male, imperial, scholar
- Stats: Cunning 5, Eloquence 4, Discretion 3, Resolve 4, Scholarship 5
- **Personality:** The most powerful man in the empire. Brother of the late Empress Zhangsun, uncle of the princes. One of Taizong's original Twenty-Four Heroes. He controls the succession — or thinks he does. He backs Chengqian out of loyalty to his sister's bloodline. He sees you as nothing.
- **Storyline hooks:** The immovable object in Act 1. In Act 2 (Zhi path), he becomes your nemesis. He's principled but blind to anyone outside the old aristocracy.

**Chu Suiliang** (褚遂良) — Gold tier
- Tags: male, noble, scholar
- Stats: Cunning 3, Eloquence 5, Discretion 2, Resolve 5, Scholarship 5
- **Personality:** Great calligrapher, rigid Confucian. He's the empire's conscience — dramatic, principled, willing to die for protocol. He manages the imperial archives. He has influence over what gets recorded.
- **Storyline hooks:** Political opponent if you break rules. Potential ally if you play the Virtue/Scholarship path. His calligraphy is a literal treasure — owning a piece is a status symbol.

**Fang Xuanling** (房玄龄) — Gold tier (DECEASED early in Act 1)
- Tags: male, noble, scholar
- **Note:** The famous chancellor dies in 648, shortly after game start. His death shifts the power balance. His faction (moderate, pragmatic) fractures. This creates openings.

**Wei Zheng** (魏征) — Gold tier (DECEASED before game start)
- Tags: male, commoner, scholar
- **Note:** The legendary remonstrator, famous for criticizing Taizong to his face. Dead before the game, but his legacy matters — he represents the ideal of honest counsel. Taizong reportedly said after Wei Zheng's death: "With him I could see my own flaws. Now that mirror is gone."

**Li Ji / Xu Shiji** (李勣 / 徐世勣) — Gold tier
- Tags: male, commoner, guard
- Stats: Cunning 4, Eloquence 2, Discretion 4, Resolve 5, Vitality 3, Resourcefulness 4. Martial 5.
- **Personality:** The greatest living general. Practical, taciturn, politically savvy. He survived every purge by knowing exactly when to stay silent. His famous quote on Wu's elevation: "This is Your Majesty's family affair — why ask outsiders?"
- **Storyline hooks:** Military kingmaker. His tacit support for any candidate is worth more than ten chancellors' endorsements. He's hard to approach but his neutrality can be tilted.

### Fictional Officials

**Secretary Liu** (刘中书) — Silver tier
- Tags: male, noble, scholar
- Stats: Cunning 3, Eloquence 4, Discretion 3, Resolve 2, Scholarship 4
- **Position:** Secretariat drafter. He writes the edicts.
- **Personality:** Cautious, follows power. Useful for forging or modifying documents. He'll do it if he thinks you'll win.

**Censor Liang** (梁御史) — Bronze tier
- Tags: male, commoner, scholar
- Stats: Cunning 4, Eloquence 3, Discretion 2, Resolve 4, Scholarship 3
- **Position:** Investigating Censor.
- **Personality:** Young, zealous, idealistic. Believes in the system. Can be a weapon or an obstacle.

**General Qin** (秦将军) — Silver tier
- Tags: male, noble, guard
- Stats: Cunning 2, Eloquence 1, Discretion 2, Resolve 4, Vitality 4, Resourcefulness 3. Martial 4.
- **Position:** Commander, Palace Guard detachment.
- **Personality:** Blunt, loyal to the throne. Whoever has his trust controls the palace physically.

## Religious Figures (Act 1)

**Xuanzang** (玄奘) — Gold tier
- Tags: male, commoner, priest
- Stats: Eloquence 5, Resolve 5, Scholarship 5, Spiritual Arts 5
- **Personality:** THE Buddhist monk. Just returned from his legendary journey to India (645). Translating sutras under imperial patronage. A living saint. Inaccessible to a Rank 9 Cairen — but if you're on the Buddhist path and have high enough Spiritual Arts, he might notice you.
- **Historical basis:** Real figure. His return was a national event.
- **Storyline hooks:** Ultimate Buddhist mentor. Meeting him is an achievement in itself.

**Taoist Master Sun** (孙道长) — Silver tier
- Tags: male, noble, priest
- Stats: Cunning 3, Eloquence 3, Discretion 4, Resolve 3, Scholarship 4, Spiritual Arts 4
- **Personality:** Court Taoist. Prepares the Emperor's longevity elixirs. He genuinely believes they work. He's not poisoning Taizong on purpose — he's killing him with faith.
- **Storyline hooks:** Taizong's health arc. You can try to expose the elixirs (buy time for the Emperor, anger the Taoists) or let them continue (Taizong dies sooner, you need him to die for your plan to work). A genuine moral dilemma.

**Nun Mingzhu** (明珠师太) — Bronze tier
- Tags: female, commoner, priest, follower
- **Note:** Temple Raised starting agent. Also present at Ganye Temple during the nunnery transition. A thread that connects both acts.

# ACT 2: VARIES BY EMPEROR

Characters who persist from Act 1 carry their relationships, conditions, and loyalty.

New characters introduced depend on which prince became emperor:

## Act 2 — Zhi Path (Historical) introduces:
- **Empress Wang** (王皇后) — Zhi's wife, your primary rival
- **Consort Xiao** (萧淑妃) — Zhi's favorite before you
- **Xu Jingzong** (许敬宗) — opportunistic chancellor, your first outer-court ally
- **Li Yifu** (李义府) — "the smiling knife," early supporter
- **Shangguan Yi** (上官仪) — chancellor who tries to depose you
- **Shangguan Wan'er** (上官婉儿) — his granddaughter, your future secretary
- **Di Renjie** (狄仁杰) — the incorruptible judge (late game)
- **Lai Junchen** (来俊臣) — secret police chief (late game)
- **Xue Huaiyi** (薛怀义) — your monk-lover (late game)
- **Zhang brothers** — your male favorites (very late game)

## Act 2 — Chengqian Path introduces:
- **Cheng Xinnu** (称心) — the prince's lover, now at court openly
- Confucian opposition faction (new characters)
- Turkic cultural advisors (new characters)
- No Empress Wang or Consort Xiao (different emperor, different wives)

## Act 2 — Tai Path introduces:
- Tai's hand-picked scholar-officials (new characters)
- His paranoia targets (characters he purges — can you save them?)
- A different set of rivals vying for a suspicious emperor's trust

## Act 2 — Dowager Path introduces:
- Whichever prince took the throne (as emperor, not as your ally)
- His wife/empress (your institutional rival)
- Your son as a political piece
