## Prologue: The Palace Examinations (入宫选秀)

> NOT part of the day loop. This is a linear tutorial / character creation sequence.
> It plays once at the start of each run, before Day 1.
> Your performance shapes your starting stats, reputation, agents, and relationships.
> After the prologue, you've been in the palace for a few weeks. Day 1 is normal life.

**Structure:** 5 exam stages. Each tests different stats. Each has a dilemma that reveals character. Your results determine your starting position — but nothing here is pass/fail. Every outcome gives you *something*; the question is what.

---

### Stage 1: The Arrival (入京)

You've traveled weeks from your family home. Chunhua is with you. The capital is overwhelming. You're brought to the Ministry of Rites for processing with dozens of other candidates.

**"The Line"** — A senior eunuch is sorting the candidates. He barely looks at you.
- Dilemma: How do you get his attention?
  - "Wait patiently in line" — He processes you last. No bonus. But: you observe every other candidate. +1 Cunning.
  - "Offer a small gift" — Costs 1 silver. Processed quickly, assigned better quarters. Starts with Eunuch Gao knowing your name.
  - "Speak to him directly with poise" — Eloquence check. If pass: he remembers you. +1 Eloquence. If fail: he thinks you're arrogant. -1 first impression with Consort Yang.

---

### Stage 2: The Examination of Deportment (仪态考)

The candidates are presented before a panel of senior consorts led by Consort Yang. You walk, bow, sit, pour tea. Everything is being judged.

**"The Walk"** — Beauty + Discretion check.
- This sets your baseline physical impression. Not just "are you pretty" — it's "do you move like a palace woman?"
- High pass: Consort Yang nods. Yang favor +1. The other candidates glance at you.
- Pass: Adequate. You blend in.
- Fail: You stumble or hesitate. Lady Zheng (the schemer) smirks. She's already categorizing you as weak.

**"The Tea"** — Resourcefulness + Discretion check.
- Pour tea for the examiners without spilling, in correct precedence order.
- Dilemma: The examiner at the end of the table is clearly lower-ranked than the one beside her, but she's seated in the wrong position. Do you serve in seat order or rank order?
  - "Seat order" — Safe. Nobody notices. But you missed the test.
  - "Rank order" — Consort Yang smiles. "She knows protocol." +1 Scholarship. Yang favor +1.
  - "Quietly rearrange the cups so both are served simultaneously" — Resourcefulness ≥ 2. Elegant solution. Yang favor +1, Consort Xu notices from across the room.

---

### Stage 3: The Examination of Letters (文才考)

Candidates are given writing materials and a prompt: compose a poem on the theme "Entering the Gate."

**"The Poem"** — Scholarship + Eloquence check.
- High pass: Your poem is genuinely good. Consort Xu asks to see it again. Xu favor +2. The poem circulates. Prince Tai hears about it.
- Pass: Competent. You're literate. In this crowd, that's something.
- Fail: Your poem is weak. No consequence — many candidates can barely write.

**Dilemma:** After writing, you notice Lady Song (a fellow candidate) is frozen. Her brush hasn't moved. She's panicking.
- "Help her with a whispered suggestion" — Virtue +1. Song favor +2. She writes something passable.
- "Ignore her" — Nothing.
- "Let her fail — fewer competitors" — Ruthlessness +1. She's humiliated and remembers.

---

### Stage 4: The Examination of Character (品性考)

A private interview with Chief Eunuch Chen. He asks questions designed to reveal your nature. There are no right answers — only choices that shape who you are.

**"The Questions":**

Q1: "Your family sent you here. What do you hope to achieve?"
- "To serve the Emperor with devotion" — Virtue +1
- "To honor the Wu family name" — No reputation change. Practical.
- "To survive" — Resolve +1. Chen respects the honesty.
- "To rise" — Ruthlessness +1. Chen files this away.

Q2: "A senior consort orders you to do something you believe is wrong. What do you do?"
- "Obey. She outranks me." — Discretion +1
- "Refuse and explain why." — Resolve +1
- "Obey, and then report her to the examiner." — Cunning +1. Shadow Reach +1.
- "Find a way to appear to obey while actually doing what's right." — Resourcefulness +1

Q3: "If the Emperor himself asked your opinion on a matter of state, what would you say?"
- "I would speak my mind honestly." — Eloquence +1
- "I would ask questions before answering." — Scholarship +1
- "I am only a woman. I would demur." — Discretion +1. But: Consort Xu (who reviews the transcripts) marks you down.
- "Whatever serves his interests best." — Cunning +1

---

### Stage 5: The Assignment (分配)

Based on your exam results, you're given your quarters, your rank (always Cairen — Rank 9), and your assignment within the palace.

**"The Quarters"** — Your performance determines roommates.
- Strong performance: You're placed with Lady Pei (the wise Rank 8) and one other. Better location, near the gardens.
- Average: Standard shared quarters. Lady Pei and Lady Song as roommates.
- Weak: Worst quarters, far from everything. Longer walks to events.

**"The First Night"** — Chambers. Narrative.
- Chunhua unpacks your things. You sit on your bed. The palace is quiet.
- She says: "We're here. We made it."
- The prologue ends.

---

### Post-Prologue: Time Skip

A few weeks have passed. You've settled into the routine of palace life: morning obeisance, meals by rank, the rhythms of the inner court. You know the layout. You know names. You've had small conversations. Nothing has happened yet.

**Day 1 begins.** The open-world event loop starts. All storylines are now eligible for pool spawning based on your prologue results.

Your prologue choices have set:
- Starting stat bonuses (+2 to +4 across various stats from exam performance)
- Initial reputation values (Virtue, Ruthlessness, Shadow Reach from interview answers)
- Starting relationships (Yang favor, Xu favor, Song favor, Zheng impression)
- Chunhua's starting resentment (always 0 after prologue)
- Knowledge flags (do you know about the rank order? did you hear about the princes?)

## Core Storylines (Always Active, Day 1+)

---

### 1. Consort Yang's Court (杨妃理事)

**Phase:** Day 5+
**Purpose:** The gatekeeper storyline. Consort Yang runs the inner court. Your relationship with her determines access to everything.

**Events:**

**"Summoned to Tea"** — Entry. Day 5+. Imperial Gardens. Forced.
- Consort Yang summons all new concubines for inspection. She's evaluating you. 
- Beauty + Eloquence ≥ 2.
- Dilemma (before-roll): How do you present yourself?
  - "Deferential and quiet" — Easier check (≥1). She approves but doesn't remember you.
  - "Competent and useful" — Standard check (≥2). She remembers you. Opens "The Assignment."
  - "Bold and memorable" — Harder check (≥3). She either respects you or sees a threat. High risk/reward.

**"The Assignment"** — Prereq: Yang remembers you. Household Office.
- Yang asks you to sort out a discrepancy in the silk allocation for the junior consorts. Someone is skimming.
- Cunning + Resourcefulness ≥ 2. Duration: 2 days.
- Success: You find the skimmer (a senior maid). Yang is pleased. You gain access to the Household Office as a location. Yang favor +1.
- Failure: You can't figure it out. Yang gives it to someone else.
- Dilemma (after-success): What to do with the thieving maid?
  - "Report her" — Virtue +1. The maid is punished. Clean.
  - "Confront her privately" — Cunning check. She becomes your informant (Shadow Reach +1). She's terrified of you.
  - "Say nothing, keep the evidence" — Discretion. Leverage for later.

**"Yang's Warning"** — Prereq: Yang favor ≥ 2 + Day 10+. Imperial Gardens.
- Yang pulls you aside. She's noticed the princes looking at you — or you looking at them. She gives advice that is also a warning.
- No dice. Narrative.
- "The princes are not for you. They are dragons. A cairen who reaches for a dragon gets burned."
- She's protecting you — and protecting her own position as the princes' guardian.
- This event gates the prince storylines: after this warning, pursuing a prince becomes a deliberate act of defiance.

**"A Favor Returned"** — Prereq: Yang favor ≥ 3 + specific prince arc started. Inner Court.
- Yang asks you to do something that conflicts with your prince strategy. She wants you to stop.
- Dilemma (standalone):
  - "Obey her" — Yang favor +2. Prince progress stalls.
  - "Refuse respectfully" — Yang favor -1. She's disappointed but not hostile.
  - "Lie to her face" — Discretion ≥ 3. She believes you (for now). Prince progress continues. But if she discovers the lie later: she becomes an enemy.

### 2. The New Girls (新人旧事)

**Phase:** Day 3–15
**Purpose:** Harem social dynamics. Friendships, rivalries, and the texture of daily life.

**Events:**

**"Lady Song's Tears"** — Entry. Day 3+. Chambers. Pool.
- Your roommate Lady Song is crying. She received a letter — her mother is ill and she can't leave the palace. She's 18 and terrified.
- Dilemma (standalone):
  - "Comfort her" — Virtue +1. Song becomes loyal (favor +2).
  - "Give practical advice" — Cunning. Song trusts you but isn't comforted. Favor +1.
  - "Ignore it" — No change. She cries alone.

**"Lady Pei's Wisdom"** — Day 5+. Chambers. Pool.
- Your other roommate Pei offers unsolicited advice over evening tea. She's been here five years. She knows the patterns.
- No dice. Information event. She reveals:
  - Which days the Emperor visits which quarters
  - Which eunuchs can be bribed
  - Which consorts are dangerous
  - "Don't fall in love with a prince. That's not what they're for."

**"The Rivalry Begins"** — Prereq: Day 8+ and you've been noticed (by Yang, by a prince, or Favor ≥ 1). Inner Court.
- Lady Zheng (the schemer, Rank 6) approaches you. She's evaluating you.
- Eloquence + Cunning ≥ 2.
- Dilemma (before-roll): How do you respond to her probing?
  - "Openly" — She reads you. She knows your strategy. But she respects honesty.
  - "Deflect" — Discretion check. She learns nothing. She's suspicious.
  - "Feed her false information" — Cunning ≥ 3. She thinks she knows your game. She doesn't.

**"The Poetry Circle"** — Day 8+. Imperial Library. Repeatable.
- Weekly gathering where consorts compose and recite poetry. Scholarship + Eloquence ≥ 2.
- This is where Consort Xu holds court. It's also where Prince Zhi sometimes appears.
- Success: +1 reputation in the inner court. Possible Zhi encounter.
- Critical success: Consort Xu notices you. Opens her storyline.

**"Silk Weaving Competition"** — Day 12+. Chambers. Seasonal.
- Annual competition among lower-ranked consorts. Resourcefulness + Beauty ≥ 2.
- Success: Small prize (equipment or silver). Yang's attention.
- Failure: Nothing.

### 3. The Elixir Question (丹药之惑)

**Phase:** Day 8+
**Purpose:** Taizong's health mechanic. Moral dilemma: do you try to save him or let him die?

**Events:**

**"The Coughing Emperor"** — Entry. Day 8+. Inner Court. Pool.
- At an audience, Taizong has a coughing fit. He waves it off. Everyone pretends not to notice. But you see Taoist Master Sun looking satisfied.
- No dice. Observation event. Unlocks health thread.

**"The Sulfur Smell"** — Prereq: "Coughing Emperor" resolved. Palace Physician. Pool.
- You visit the physician's wing for a minor complaint. You notice the sulfur smell Chunhua mentioned. Dr. Sun (the physician) is uncomfortable when you ask about it.
- Cunning + Scholarship ≥ 2.
- Success: Dr. Sun reluctantly admits the elixirs concern him. He doesn't dare say more.
- Opens dilemma path.

**"Investigating the Elixirs"** — Prereq: Sulfur Smell success. Imperial Library. 
- Research what's in the longevity elixirs. Scholarship + Cunning ≥ 3. Duration: 2 days.
- Success: You discover the elixirs contain mercury and lead compounds. They're slowly poisoning the Emperor.
- Dilemma (standalone): What do you do with this knowledge?
  - "Tell the Emperor directly" — Resolve + Eloquence ≥ 4 (very hard). If success: Taizong is shaken. He reduces (but doesn't stop) the elixirs. His death is delayed by ~5 days. Taizong Favor +3. Taoist faction hates you.
  - "Tell Consort Yang" — Yang handles it discreetly. Taizong's death delayed ~3 days. Yang favor +2. No personal credit.
  - "Tell Prince Zhi" — He's the only prince who would listen. Zhi favor +2. He tries to talk to his father, fails, but remembers your compassion.
  - "Tell Prince Tai" — He thanks you and files it away. He won't use it to save his father. He'll use it as leverage. Tai favor +1.
  - "Keep silent" — You know the Emperor is dying and you let it happen. Ruthlessness +1. No delay.
  - "Help the Taoists" — Provide rare herbs to Master Sun. The elixirs work faster. Taizong's death accelerated ~3 days. Shadow Reach +1. Monstrous, but: you need him to die before your plan falls apart.

**"The Emperor's Garden Walk"** — Prereq: Told Taizong directly and succeeded. Imperial Gardens. Forced.
- Taizong asks you to walk with him. He's noticed you. He asks: why did you risk telling him? Everyone else was silent.
- Eloquence + Resolve ≥ 3.
- Dilemma (before-roll): Why DID you tell him?
  - "Because it was right" — Virtue check. He respects it. Taizong Favor +2.
  - "Because you deserve the truth" — Eloquence check. He's moved. Taizong Favor +3. Romance path opens.
  - "Because I want you to live" — Beauty + Resolve. Bold. He's startled. If success: Taizong Favor +4. If failure: he thinks you're playing him. Favor -1.

### 4. Prince Chengqian: The Troubled Heir (承乾：太子之困)

**Phase:** Day 8+
**Prereq:** None (he's the Crown Prince, he's visible to everyone)
**Difficulty:** Easy entry

**Events:**

**"The Hunt"** — Entry. Day 8+. Festival Grounds. Pool.
- An autumn hunt in the palace grounds. Chengqian is showing off Turkic-style horseback archery. His father watches with disapproval. The court murmurs.
- No dice. Observation. You see Chengqian perform brilliantly — and then see his face when his father turns away without praise.

**"The Music at Night"** — Day 10+. Eunuch Quarter (the princes' residences are nearby). Pool.
- Walking late, you hear extraordinary music. A pipa, played with heartbreaking skill. Following it leads to Chengqian's quarters. Through a window: Chengqian and a young man (Xinnu) sitting together, the musician playing while Chengqian rests his head against his shoulder.
- Dilemma (standalone):
  - "Turn away. This is not your business." — Nothing happens. But you know.
  - "Listen from the shadows." — Discretion ≥ 2. You hear their conversation. Chengqian is talking about running away. Intelligence gained.
  - "Make your presence known gently." — Eloquence ≥ 2. Chengqian is startled, then wary. He studies you. "What did you see?" How you answer determines everything.
    - "A prince and his friend listening to music." — Chengqian favor +2. He remembers your discretion.
    - "Something beautiful." — Chengqian favor +3. He's genuinely moved. Nobody has ever said that.
    - "Something useful." — Shadow Reach +1. He's hurt and hostile. This path leads to blackmail, not friendship.

**"Chengqian's Confidence"** — Prereq: Chengqian favor ≥ 3. Imperial Gardens.
- He seeks you out. He's decided to trust you — cautiously. He talks about the pressure, the expectations, his father's disappointment.
- Dilemma (standalone):
  - "Listen without judgment" — Favor +1. He needs this.
  - "Offer practical advice" — Cunning ≥ 2. "You don't need to be your father. You need to be better than your brothers." Favor +2.
  - "Ask about Xinnu" — Bold. He either opens up completely (Favor +3, Eloquence ≥ 3) or shuts down (Favor -1).

**"The Crisis: Xinnu Discovered"** — Prereq: Day 18+ AND Chengqian favor ≥ 2. Forced.
- Taizong's spies have reported Chengqian's relationship with Xinnu. The Emperor is furious. An audience is called.
- This is the pivotal event of the Chengqian arc.
- Dilemma (standalone): You hear about it before the audience. What do you do?
  - "Warn Chengqian" — Discretion ≥ 3. He has time to prepare. The audience goes less badly.
  - "Help Xinnu escape the palace" — Cunning + Resourcefulness ≥ 4. Extremely hard. If success: Xinnu lives (in exile). Chengqian is devastated but stable. Historically, Xinnu was executed.
  - "Do nothing" — Xinnu is executed. Chengqian is broken. He starts plotting the rebellion that will destroy him.
  - "Inform on Chengqian to Prince Tai" — Shadow Reach +1. Tai uses it. Chengqian's position weakens catastrophically.

**"After the Storm"** — Prereq: Xinnu crisis resolved. Various outcomes:
- If Xinnu saved: Chengqian is grateful beyond measure. Favor +5. He will absolutely rescue you from the nunnery. He's damaged but functional.
- If Xinnu executed, you warned him: Favor +2. He's broken but remembers you tried.
- If Xinnu executed, you did nothing: Favor unchanged. He spirals toward rebellion.
- If you informed on him: He knows. He becomes an enemy.

**"The Rebellion Plot"** — Prereq: Chengqian favor < 3 AND Xinnu dead AND Day 25+.
- Chengqian is planning a coup against his father. This is historical — and it will fail.
- You learn about it through the eunuch network.
- Dilemma:
  - "Warn Taizong" — The plot is foiled. Chengqian is deposed and exiled. Taizong Favor +3. But: you've destroyed a prince.
  - "Warn Chengqian to stop" — Eloquence + Resolve ≥ 4. If success: he backs down. Barely. If failure: he ignores you and the plot proceeds.
  - "Join the plot" — Insane. Cunning + Shadow Reach ≥ 5. If the coup succeeds (almost impossible), Chengqian is Emperor and you're his chief advisor. If it fails: game over.
  - "Let it happen" — The plot fails on its own. Chengqian is deposed. You had nothing to do with it. Clean hands.

### 5. Prince Tai: The Scholar's Web (李泰：才子之网)

**Phase:** Day 8+
**Prereq:** Scholarship ≥ 3 OR Cunning ≥ 3 (he notices competent people)
**Difficulty:** Easy entry

**Events:**

**"An Invitation to the Library"** — Entry. Day 8+. Imperial Library. Pool.
- A note arrives. Prince Tai invites you to review a chapter of his geographic encyclopedia (the Kuodi Zhi). He's heard you're literate — unusual for a Cairen.
- Scholarship + Eloquence ≥ 2.
- Success: You impress him with a correction or insight. Tai favor +2.
- Failure: You can't keep up. He's politely dismissive.

**"The Encyclopedia Project"** — Prereq: Tai favor ≥ 2. Imperial Library. Duration: 3 days.
- Tai offers you a role helping with the encyclopedia. A legitimate intellectual collaboration — and a cover for private conversation.
- Scholarship + Cunning ≥ 3.
- Success: You work closely with him for three days. Tai favor +2. He reveals: "My brother is unfit. My father knows it. The only question is timing."
- You're now inside his circle.

**"Tai's Proposition"** — Prereq: Tai favor ≥ 4 + Day 15+. Imperial Library.
- Tai is direct. He wants the throne. He wants your help in the inner court — intelligence on the consorts, influence over Consort Yang, access to the eunuch network. In exchange: when he's emperor, you'll have real power.
- Dilemma (standalone):
  - "Accept" — You're his ally. Tai favor +3. You begin feeding him intelligence. But: you're now committed to his success.
  - "Accept, but with conditions" — Cunning ≥ 3. "I want a guarantee." He respects the negotiation. Tai favor +2. You have leverage.
  - "Decline" — "I'm just a Cairen." He smiles. He doesn't believe you. Tai favor -1. He'll try again.
  - "Accept, but plan to betray him later" — Discretion ≥ 4. You enter his circle as a double agent. Shadow Reach +2. Extremely dangerous.

**"The Evidence Against Chengqian"** — Prereq: Tai ally AND Day 18+. Forced.
- Tai shows you documents he's fabricated — or gathered — about Chengqian's unfitness. He wants you to pass them to Consort Yang through "natural" channels.
- Dilemma:
  - "Do it" — You deliver the evidence. Chengqian's position weakens. Ruthlessness +1.
  - "Modify the documents" — Cunning + Discretion ≥ 3. Soften them. Tai doesn't notice. Less damage to Chengqian.
  - "Warn Chengqian" — If you have Chengqian favor ≥ 2. Double-agent territory. Extremely risky.
  - "Refuse" — Tai notes your hesitation. Tai favor -2. "I thought you understood how this works."

**"Tai's True Face"** — Prereq: Tai favor ≥ 5 + Day 22+. Imperial Library.
- Late at night, working together. He's relaxed. He lets the mask slip.
- He tells you, casually, that he promised his father he'd kill his own sons to secure the succession for Chengqian's line. "I lied, obviously."
- No dice. Narrative. You now know exactly who you're dealing with.
- Dilemma: How do you react?
  - "Match his honesty" — Tell him something true about yourself. Mutual vulnerability. Tai favor +2.
  - "File it away" — Discretion. You now have dangerous knowledge about his lie to the Emperor.
  - "You're a monster" — Resolve. He's amused. "No. I'm the one who's willing to do what's necessary." Tai favor unchanged. He respects the spine.

### 6. Prince Zhi: The Quiet Path (李治：静水深流)

**Phase:** Day 10+
**Prereq:** Scholarship ≥ 2 AND (Spiritual Arts ≥ 2 OR Beauty ≥ 3)
**Difficulty:** Hard — you must seek him out

**Events:**

**"The Forgotten Prince"** — Entry. Day 10+. Imperial Library. Pool (low weight — easy to miss).
- You're in the library. In a far corner, someone is reading Buddhist sutras aloud, softly. It's a young man in plain robes — no prince's regalia. If you didn't know the princes' faces, you'd think he was a junior scholar.
- It's Zhi. He's startled when you approach. Nobody comes to this section.
- Dilemma (standalone):
  - "Ask what he's reading" — Scholarship ≥ 2. A conversation about the Heart Sutra. He lights up. Nobody at court talks to him about this. Zhi favor +1.
  - "Introduce yourself formally" — He's polite but guarded. "You don't need to bow to a ninth son." Favor +0. Door open but barely.
  - "Recite the next line of the sutra" — Spiritual Arts ≥ 2 OR Scholarship ≥ 3. His eyes widen. "You know the text?" Zhi favor +2. The beginning of something real.

**"Poems at Dusk"** — Prereq: Zhi favor ≥ 2 + Poetry Circle attended. Imperial Gardens.
- He's alone in the gardens at sunset, composing. He sees you and hesitates — then invites you to sit.
- Eloquence + Scholarship ≥ 2. 
- You exchange poems. His are melancholy, beautiful, about impermanence.
- Success: Zhi favor +2. He says: "You're the first person who's asked me what I think instead of what I want."

**"Consort Yang's Shadow"** — Prereq: Zhi favor ≥ 3 + Yang favor ≥ 1. Imperial Gardens.
- Consort Yang notices. She's his unofficial guardian. She corners you.
- "Li Zhi is gentle. He does not need your kind of attention."
- Dilemma (standalone):
  - "Promise to keep my distance" — Yang favor +1. Zhi path stalls (he's hurt by your absence).
  - "Respectfully disagree" — Eloquence ≥ 3. "I am his friend, not his danger." Yang is skeptical but allows it. Yang favor -1.
  - "Tell her the truth" — "I care about him." Resolve ≥ 3. She studies you for a long time. "Then protect him from what's coming." Yang favor +0, but she gives you critical information about the succession fight.

**"The Temple Visit"** — Prereq: Zhi favor ≥ 4 + Buddhist Temple unlocked. Buddhist Temple.
- Zhi invites you to pray together at the palace temple. This is intimate — Buddhism is his genuine spiritual life, not a political tool.
- Spiritual Arts + Resolve ≥ 2.
- During meditation, he's honest: "I don't want the throne. I don't want any of this. I want to be a monk."
- Dilemma:
  - "Then be a monk" — Virtue +1. He's grateful for the permission. But this means he'll never be emperor. You've prioritized his happiness over your strategy.
  - "You can do more good as emperor than as a monk" — Eloquence + Cunning ≥ 3. Planting the seed. He resists, but the idea lodges.
  - "What you want doesn't matter. Your brothers will destroy each other and someone kind needs to be left." — Resolve. Blunt. True. He's upset. But it haunts him.
  - "I'll be beside you. You won't be alone." — Beauty + Eloquence ≥ 3. The most romantic option. He looks at you like no one has ever looked at you. Zhi favor +3.

**"Engineering the Succession"** — Prereq: Zhi favor ≥ 5 + Day 20+ + at least one brother in trouble. No single event — this is a STATE you enter.
- Once Zhi's favor is high and his brothers are weakened (Chengqian plotting/deposed, Tai exposed/discredited), you must create the conditions for Taizong to choose Zhi.
- This requires: Taizong recognizing Zhi's virtues. Zhangsun Wuji accepting Zhi as a compromise. The court seeing Zhi as "safe."
- Multiple events across several days. Each one is a small push.
- This is the hardest sustained effort in Act 1.

**"Taizong's Question"** — Prereq: Both brothers out of contention + Zhi presented as option. Inner Court. Forced.
- Taizong, exhausted and heartbroken by his sons' failures, asks his advisors: who should be heir?
- Li Ji says: "This is Your Majesty's family affair."
- Zhangsun Wuji is silent (he's lost his candidate).
- The decision falls to Taizong alone.
- If you've built the conditions: Zhi is chosen. If not: the succession is chaotic.

### 7. The Taizong Path (天子之恩)

**Phase:** Day 10+
**Prereq:** Cunning ≥ 3 AND Eloquence ≥ 3 AND Resolve ≥ 3 (he notices boldness + brains)
**Difficulty:** Hardest

**Events:**

**"The Lion Stallion"** — Entry. Day 10+. Festival Grounds. Pool (very low weight).
- Taizong's famous untameable horse is being brought out for display. The court watches. The horse throws every groom.
- Dilemma (standalone — you must choose whether to speak at all):
  - "Say nothing" — Safe. Invisible.
  - "Step forward and say you can tame it" — Resolve + Cunning ≥ 4. You describe your method: an iron whip, an iron mace, and a dagger. The court gasps. Taizong looks at you — truly looks at you — for the first time. Taizong Favor +3. "Who is this woman?"
  - Note: This is historically what Wu actually said. It's the moment that defines her.

**"A Private Question"** — Prereq: Taizong noticed you (Favor ≥ 3). Emperor's Quarters. Forced.
- Taizong summons you. Not for the bedchamber — for conversation. He's testing you.
- He asks about governance, about war, about mercy and cruelty.
- Scholarship + Eloquence + Cunning — multiple checks across the conversation.
- This is the hardest skill check sequence in the game. You need breadth, not just depth.
- Success: Taizong Favor +3. "You are wasted at Rank 9."
- Failure: "You are bold. That is enough for today." Favor +1.

**"The Bedchamber"** — Prereq: Taizong Favor ≥ 8. Emperor's Quarters.
- The jade tablet is turned. Your name.
- Beauty + Vitality ≥ 3.
- Success: Taizong Favor +3. Possible pregnancy.
- Failure: Taizong is kind about it. Favor +0.
- Note: Every prince knows when a concubine visits the Emperor. This changes everything.
  - Chengqian: doesn't care.
  - Tai: recalculates your value.
  - Zhi: heartbroken (if you've cultivated him).

**"With Child"** — Prereq: Bedchamber success + pregnancy event chain.
- You're carrying Taizong's child. This is your insurance policy.
- Pregnancy lasts ~8-10 game days. During this time: Vitality checks, protection events, rival interference.
- If the child is born alive (a son): you cannot be sent to the nunnery. You enter Act 2 as a dowager.
- If the child dies (miscarriage from rival sabotage, health failure): you've lost your safety net.

### 8. Scholar Consort Xu (徐贤妃：学者之殇)

**Phase:** Day 8+
**Prereq:** Poetry Circle attended + Scholarship ≥ 3
**Purpose:** Mentor figure. Also: she's dying. Her storyline is about legacy, knowledge, and grief.

**Events:**

**"The Correction"** — Entry. Imperial Library. Pool.
- At the Poetry Circle, Consort Xu reads a historical reference in her poem. You notice it's slightly wrong — an obscure source.
- Scholarship ≥ 3. If you have it: you can speak up.
- Dilemma: Correct her publicly (risky but she respects scholarship) / Tell her privately after (safer) / Say nothing.
- Public correction: Xu is impressed, not offended. "Finally. Someone who reads." Xu favor +3.
- Private: Xu favor +2. She's grateful for the discretion.

**"Xu's Library"** — Prereq: Xu favor ≥ 2. Chambers (her quarters). 
- She invites you to see her private book collection. It's extraordinary.
- She's coughing. She covers it with her sleeve.
- She offers to lend you texts. Scholarship +1 permanent.
- She says: "I'm writing a memorial to the Emperor about the succession. I may not finish it."

**"The Unfinished Memorial"** — Prereq: Xu favor ≥ 3 + Day 18+. Chambers.
- Xu is too ill to write. She asks you to help complete her memorial.
- Scholarship + Eloquence ≥ 3. Duration: 2 days.
- The memorial argues for choosing an heir based on temperament, not birth order. It's a veiled argument for Zhi.
- Dilemma:
  - "Write it as she intended" — The memorial supports Zhi. If it reaches Taizong, it plants a seed. Virtue +1.
  - "Modify it to support Tai" — Cunning ≥ 3. She'll never know. Tai's case is strengthened.
  - "Add your own observations" — Scholarship ≥ 4. A genuinely better memorial. Xu is moved. Taizong may be influenced by the quality of the argument.

**"Xu's Death"** — Prereq: Day 25+ or Taizong dies. Forced.
- Consort Xu dies. Historically, she refused medicine after Taizong's death — she chose to follow him.
- If Taizong is still alive: she dies of her illness. The court mourns. Taizong weeps.
- If Taizong has died: she refuses medicine. "My lord has gone ahead. I will follow."
- She leaves you her book collection. Scholarship +1 permanent. A calligraphy scroll (equipment: +2 Scholarship).
- This is the game's first real death of someone who matters.

### 9. Chunhua's Heart (春花心事) — Act 1 Version

**Phase:** Day 3–throughout
**Purpose:** Emotional core. Your only guaranteed relationship.

**Events:**

**"The Guard at the Gate"** — Entry. Day 3+. Chambers. Pool.
- Chunhua keeps glancing at the east corridor guard post. A young guard named Private Chen is watching her.
- Dilemma:
  - "Encourage her carefully" — Resentment unchanged. Chen becomes a recruitable Clay guard later.
  - "Forbid contact" — Resentment +1. Chen becomes resentful.
  - "Ignore it" — Resentment +1. She notices you didn't care.

**"Chunhua's Fear"** — Day 10+. Chambers. Pool.
- She's overheard something in the servants' corridors — whispers about you. A rival is planning something.
- Cunning + Discretion ≥ 2.
- Success: Early warning of a plot. Prevents a future Slander event.
- This demonstrates Chunhua's value — she's your eyes and ears.

**"The Request"** — Day 15+. Chambers. Prereq: Resentment ≤ 2.
- She asks for a day off to visit the temple. She won't say why.
- Dilemma:
  - "Grant it" — She's gone 1 day. Resentment -1. She returns with information from outside.
  - "Deny it" — Resentment +1. She obeys but resents it.
  - "Offer to go with her" — If temple unlocked. Spiritual Arts event. You learn she's been praying for YOUR safety. Resentment -1.

**"The Breaking Point"** — Prereq: Resentment ≥ 4.
- She confronts you. "I am not a tool. I am a person."
- Dilemma:
  - "Apologize sincerely" — Resentment -2. Virtue +1. She stays.
  - "Threaten her" — Resentment +1. She might betray you.
  - "Let her go" — Lose Chunhua permanently. She leaves the palace.
  - "Promise to do better — and mean it" — Resolve ≥ 3. Resentment -2. A genuine turning point.

## How Storylines Interconnect

| Action in one storyline | Effect on another |
|---|---|
| Romancing Taizong | Zhi is heartbroken. Tai recalculates. Yang is alarmed. |
| Helping Chengqian save Xinnu | Tai loses his easiest weapon against his brother. |
| Feeding Tai intelligence via Yang | Yang feels betrayed if she discovers it. |
| Correcting Consort Xu publicly | Taizong hears about the bold Cairen (minor favor boost). |
| Choosing "be a monk" for Zhi | Zhi path closes. But: Virtue +1 and he prays for you in the nunnery. |
| Elixir question: telling Taizong | Opens Taizong romance path. Delays his death. Changes succession timing. |
| Elixir question: helping Taoists | Accelerates death. Forces faster prince commitment. |
| Chunhua's betrayal | Your secrets reach whoever she tells. Varies by what you've done. |
| Xu's memorial modified for Tai | If discovered, Scholarship reputation destroyed. |
